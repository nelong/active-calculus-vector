Activity 9.1.8.

  1. Let \(f(x,y) = x^2+y^2\text{.}\) Draw the level curves \(f(x,y) = k\) for \(k=1\text{,}\) \(k=2\text{,}\) \(k=3\text{,}\) and \(k=4\) on the left set of axes given in Figure 9.1.17. (You decide on the scale of the axes.) Explain what the surface defined by \(f\) looks like.

  2. Let \(g(x,y) = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\text{.}\) Draw the level curves \(g(x,y) = k\) for \(k=1\text{,}\) \(k=2\text{,}\) \(k=3\text{,}\) and \(k=4\) on the right set of axes given in Figure 9.1.17. (You decide on the scale of the axes.) Explain what the surface defined by \(g\) looks like.

  3. Compare and contrast the graphs of \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\) How are they alike? How are they different? Use traces for each function to help answer these questions.
