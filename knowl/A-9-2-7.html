Activity 9.2.5.

  1. Let \(A = (2,3)\) and \(B = (4,7)\text{,}\) as shown at left in Figure 9.2.9. Compute \(|\overrightarrow{AB}|\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(\vv = \langle v_1, v_2 \rangle\) be the vector in \(\R^2\) with components \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) as shown at right in Figure 9.2.9. Use the distance formula to find a general formula for \(|\vv|\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(\vv = \langle v_1, v_2, v_3 \rangle\) be a vector in \(\R^3\text{.}\) Use the distance formula to find a general formula for \(|\vv|\text{.}\)
  4. Suppose that \(\vu = \langle 2,3\rangle\) and \(\vv = \langle -1,2\rangle\text{.}\) Find \(|\vu|\text{,}\) \(|\vv|\text{,}\) and \(|\vu+\vv|\text{.}\) Is it true that \(|\vu + \vv| = |\vu|+|\vv|\text{?}\)
  5. Under what conditions will \(|\vu+\vv| = |\vu|+|\vv|\text{?}\) (Hint: Think about how \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu+\vv\) form the sides of a triangle.)
  6. With the vector \(\vu = \langle 2,3\rangle\text{,}\) find the lengths of \(2\vu\text{,}\) \(3\vu\text{,}\) and \(-2\vu\text{,}\) respectively, and use proper notation to label your results.
  7. If \(t\) is any scalar, how is \(|t\vu|\) related to \(|\vu|\text{?}\)
  8. A unit vector is a vector whose magnitude is 1. Of the vectors \(\textbf{i}\text{,}\) \(\textbf{j}\text{,}\) and \(\textbf{i} + \textbf{ j}\text{,}\) which are unit vectors?
  9. Find a unit vector \(\vv\) whose direction is the same as \(\vu = \langle 2, 3\rangle\text{.}\) (Hint: Consider the result of part (g).)
