Activity 10.2.2.

Consider the function \(f\) defined by
\begin{equation*} f(x,y) = \frac{xy^2}{x+1} \end{equation*}
at the point \((1,2)\text{.}\)
  1. Write the trace \(f(x,2)\) at the fixed value \(y=2\text{.}\) On the left side of Figure 10.2.5, draw the graph of the trace with \(y=2\) around the point where \(x=1\text{,}\) indicating the scale and labels on the axes. Also, sketch the tangent line at the point \(x=1\text{.}\)
  2. Find the partial derivative \(f_x(1,2)\) and relate its value to the sketch you just made.
  3. Write the trace \(f(1,y)\) at the fixed value \(x=1\text{.}\) On the right side of Figure 10.2.5, draw the graph of the trace with \(x=1\) indicating the scale and labels on the axes. Also, sketch the tangent line at the point \(y=2\text{.}\)
  4. Find the partial derivative \(f_y(1,2)\) and relate its value to the sketch you just made.
