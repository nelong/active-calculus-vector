Activity 10.2.4.

The speed of sound \(C\) traveling through ocean water is a function of temperature, salinity and depth. It may be modeled by the function
\begin{equation*} C=1449.2+4.6T-0.055T^2+0.00029T^3+(1.34-0.01T)(S-35)+0.016D. \end{equation*}
Here \(C\) is the speed of sound in meters/second, \(T\) is the temperature in degrees Celsius, \(S\) is the salinity in grams/liter of water, and \(D\) is the depth below the ocean surface in meters.
  1. State the units in which each of the partial derivatives, \(C_T\text{,}\) \(C_S\) and \(C_D\text{,}\) are expressed and explain the physical meaning of each.
  2. Find the partial derivatives \(C_T\text{,}\) \(C_S\) and \(C_D\text{.}\)
  3. Evaluate each of the three partial derivatives at the point where \(T=10\text{,}\) \(S=35\) and \(D=100\text{.}\) What does the sign of each partial derivatives tell us about the behavior of the function \(C\) at the point \((10,35, 100)\text{?}\)
