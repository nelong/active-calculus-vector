Activity 11.7.2.

  1. Set up and evaluate the triple integral of \(f(x,y,z) = x-y+2z\) over the box \(B = [-2,3] \times [1,4] \times [0,2]\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(S\) be the solid cone bounded by \(z = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) and \(z=3\text{.}\) A picture of \(S\) is shown at right in Figure 11.7.4. Our goal in what follows is to set up an iterated integral of the form
    \begin{equation} \int_{x=?}^{x=?} \int_{y=?}^{y=?} \int_{z=?}^{z=?} \delta(x,y,z) \, dz \, dy \, dx\tag{11.7.1} \end{equation}
    to represent the mass of \(S\) in the setting where \(\delta(x,y,z)\) tells us the density of \(S\) at the point \((x,y,z)\text{.}\) Our particular task is to find the limits on each of the three integrals.
    1. If we think about slicing up the solid, we can consider slicing the domain of the solid’s projection onto the \(xy\)-plane (just as we would slice a two-dimensional region in \(\R^2\)), and then slice in the \(z\)-direction as well. The projection of the solid onto the \(xy\)-plane is shown at left in Figure 11.7.4. If we decide to first slice the domain of the solid’s projection perpendicular to the \(x\)-axis, over what range of constant \(x\)-values would we have to slice?
    2. If we continue with slicing the domain, what are the limits on \(y\) on a typical slice? How do these depend on \(x\text{?}\) What, therefore, are the limits on the middle integral?
    3. Finally, now that we have thought about slicing up the two-dimensional domain that is the projection of the cone, what are the limits on \(z\) in the innermost integral? Note that over any point \((x,y)\) in the plane, a vertical slice in the \(z\) direction will involve a range of values from the cone itself to its flat top. In particular, observe that at least one of these limits is not constant but depends on \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)
    4. In conclusion, write an iterated integral of the form (11.7.1) that represents the mass of the cone \(S\text{.}\)
