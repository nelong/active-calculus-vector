Activity 9.5.5.

Let \(P_0 = (1,2,-1)\text{,}\) \(P_1 = (1, 0 ,-1)\text{,}\) and \(P_2 = (0,1,3)\) and let \(p\) be the plane containing \(P_0\text{,}\) \(P_1\text{,}\) and \(P_2\text{.}\)

  1. Determine the components of the vectors \(\overrightarrow{P_0P_1}\) and \(\overrightarrow{P_0P_2}\text{.}\)

  2. Find a normal vector \(\vn\) to the plane \(p\text{.}\)

  3. Find a scalar equation of the plane \(p\text{.}\)

  4. Consider a second plane, \(q\text{,}\) with scalar equation \(-3(x-1) + 4(y+3) + 2(z-5)=0\text{.}\) Find two different points on plane \(q\text{,}\) as well as a vector \(\vm\) that is normal to \(q\text{.}\)

  5. The angle between two planes is the acute angle between their respective normal vectors. What is the angle between planes \(p\) and \(q\text{?}\)
