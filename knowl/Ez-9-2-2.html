Exercise 14.

Let \(\vu = \langle 2, 1 \rangle\) and \(\vv = \langle 1, 2 \rangle\text{.}\)
  1. Determine the components and draw geometric representations of the vectors \(2\vu\text{,}\) \(\frac{1}{2}\vu\text{,}\) \((-1)\vu\text{,}\) and \((-3)\vu\) on the same set of axes.
  2. Determine the components and draw geometric representations of the vectors \(\vu + \vv\text{,}\) \(\vu + 2\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu + 3\vv\) on the same set of axes.
  3. Determine the components and draw geometric representations of the vectors \(\vu - \vv\text{,}\) \(\vu - 2\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu - 3\vv\) on the same set of axes.
  4. Recall that \(\vu - \vv = \vu + (-1)\vv\text{.}\) Sketch the vectors \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\text{,}\) \(\vu+\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu-\vv\) on the same set of axes. Use the “tip to tail” perspective for vector addition to explain the geometric relationship between \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu - \vv\text{.}\)
