Exercise 15.

Recall that given any vector \(\vv\text{,}\) we can calculate its length, \(|\vv|\text{.}\) Also, we say that two vectors that are scalar multiples of one another are parallel.

  1. Let \(\vv = \langle 3,4 \rangle\) in \(\R^2\text{.}\) Compute \(|\vv|\text{,}\) and determine the components of the vector \(\vu = \frac{1}{|\vv|} \vv\text{.}\) What is the magnitude of the vector \(\vu\text{?}\) How does its direction compare to \(\vv\text{?}\)

  2. Let \(\vw = 3\vi - 3\vj\) in \(\R^2\text{.}\) Determine a unit vector \(\vu\) in the same direction as \(\vw\text{.}\)

  3. Let \(\vv = \langle 2, 3, 5 \rangle\) in \(\R^3\text{.}\) Compute \(|\vv|\text{,}\) and determine the components of the vector \(\vu = \frac{1}{|\vv|} \vv\text{.}\) What is the magnitude of the vector \(\vu\text{?}\) How does its direction compare to \(\vv\text{?}\)

  4. Let \(\vv\) be an arbitrary nonzero vector in \(\R^3\text{.}\) Write a general formula for a unit vector that is parallel to \(\vv\text{.}\)
