Exercise 11.

Shown in Figure 10.3.9 is a contour plot of a function \(f\) with the values of \(f\) labeled on the contours. The point \((2,1)\) is highlighted in red.
  1. Estimate the partial derivatives \(f_x(2,1)\) and \(f_y(2,1)\text{.}\)
  2. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(f_{xx}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  3. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(f_{yy}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  4. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(f_{xy}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  5. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(f_{yx}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  6. Consider a function \(g\) of the variables \(x\) and \(y\) for which \(g_x(2,2) > 0\) and \(g_{xx}(2,2) \lt 0\text{.}\) Sketch possible behavior of some contours around \((2,2)\) on the left axes in Figure 10.3.10.
  7. Consider a function \(h\) of the variables \(x\) and \(y\) for which \(h_x(2,2) > 0\) and \(h_{xy}(2,2) \lt 0\text{.}\) Sketch possible behavior of some contour lines around \((2,2)\) on the right axes in Figure 10.3.10.
