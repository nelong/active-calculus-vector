Exercise 4.

Are the following statements true or false?
  1. If \(S\) is the cube bounded by the six planes \(x = \pm 3\text{,}\) \(y = \pm 3\text{,}\) \(z = \pm 3\text{,}\) oriented outward, and \(\vec{F} = 2 \vec{i} - \vec{k}\text{,}\) then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) through \(S\) is zero.
  2. If \(S\) is the unit sphere centered at the origin, oriented outward and \(\vec{F} = x \vec{i} + y \vec{j} + z\vec{k} = \vec{r}\text{,}\) then the flux integral \(\displaystyle \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A}\) is positive.
  3. If \(S\) is an open-ended circular cylinder centered about the z-axis, oriented away from the z-axis, and \(\vec{F} = \langle 3,-2,6 \rangle\text{,}\) then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) through \(S\) is zero.
  4. If \(S\) is the unit sphere centered at the origin, oriented outward and the flux integral \(\displaystyle \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A}\) is zero, then \(\vec{F}(x,y,z)\) is perpendicular to \(\vec{r} = \langle x,y,z \rangle\) at every point of \(S\text{.}\)
  5. The area vector of a flat, oriented surface is parallel to the surface.
  6. If \(S_1\) is a rectangle with area 1 and \(S_2\) is a rectangle with area 2, then \(\displaystyle 2 \iint_{S_1} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \iint_{S_2} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A}\)
  7. If \(S\) is an open-ended circular cylinder centered about the z-axis, oriented away from the z-axis, and \(\vec{F} = \langle x,y,0 \rangle\text{,}\) then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) through \(S\) is positive.
  8. If \(S\) is the unit sphere centered at the origin, oriented outward and the flux integral \(\displaystyle \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A}\) is zero, then \(\vec{F} = \vec{0}\text{.}\)
