Activity 12.5.3.

In this activity, we will examine why we must be careful when using symmetry to make arguments about scalar line integral. Let \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) be the paths shown in Figure 12.5.16. We will consider the function \(f(x,y)=x\) for this activity.
(a)
Parameterize \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) as \(\vr_1(t)\) and \(\vr_2(t)\text{.}\) (It is fine to have \(0\leq t\leq 1\) for both of your parameterizations.)
(b)
Use Theorem 12.5.11 to compute \(\displaystyle\int_{C_1} f\, ds\) and \(\displaystyle\int_{C_2} f\, ds\text{.}\)
(c)
As with line integrals of vector fields, we have that \(\displaystyle\int_{C_1+C_2} f\, ds = \int_{C_1} f\, ds + \int_{C_2} f\, ds\text{.}\) Use this property to compute \(\displaystyle\int_{C_1+C_2} f\, ds\text{.}\)
