Example 12.7.10.

In this example, we will look at \(\vF=\langle 0,x,0\rangle \text{.}\) Applying Definition 12.7.9,
\begin{equation*} \curl(\vF)= \left\langle \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(0) -\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(x), - \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(0)-\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(0) \right) , \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (x)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(0) \right\rangle \end{equation*}
Thus, \(\curl(\vF)=\langle 0,0,1\rangle\text{.}\) This means that \(\vF\) will have a rotational aspect only with an axis parallel to the \(z\)-axis. This should not be suprising since the plot of \(\vF\) will be the same as Figure 12.7.4 copied on each plane \(z=c\text{.}\) As you saw in Preview Activity 12.7.1, this vector field will have constant rotational strength at every point with axis of rotation in the \(\vk\) direction.
