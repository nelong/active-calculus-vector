Example 12.7.13.

In this example, we will look at \(\vH=\langle x-y,y+2z,x^2\rangle \text{.}\) By Definition 12.7.9,
\begin{align*} \curl(\vH)= \amp \left\langle \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x^2) -\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(y+2z), - \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^2)-\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(x-y)\right) ,\right.\\ \amp \left.\frac{\partial}{\partial x} (y+2z)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x-y) \right\rangle \end{align*}
Therefore, \(\curl(\vH)=\langle 0,2x,-1\rangle\text{.}\) This tells us that the vector field \(\vH\) will have a non-zero curl for every point. However, it will be quite difficult to “see” the curl at a particular point.
