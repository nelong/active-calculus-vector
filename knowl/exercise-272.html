Exercise 10.

The temperature at any point on a metal plate in the \(xy\) plane is given by \(T(x,y) = 100-4x^2 - y^2\text{,}\) where \(x\) and \(y\) are measured in inches and \(T\) in degrees Celsius. Consider the portion of the plate that lies on the rectangular region \(R = [1,5] \times [3,6]\text{.}\)
  1. Estimate the value of \(\iint_R T(x,y) \, dA\) by using a double Riemann sum with two subintervals in each direction and choosing \((x_i^*, y_j^*)\) to be the point that lies in the upper right corner of each subrectangle.
  2. Determine the area of the rectangle \(R\text{.}\)
  3. Estimate the average temperature, \(T_{\operatorname{AVG}(R)}\text{,}\) over the region \(R\text{.}\)
  4. Do you think your estimate in (c) is an over- or under-estimate of the true temperature? Why?
