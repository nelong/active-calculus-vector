Example 12.9.8.

In this example we will compute the flux of the vector field \(\vF= \langle xz-2, y+x, 1 \rangle \) over the surface of the cone given by \(x^2+y^2=z^2\) with \(1\leq z \leq 3\text{.}\) The first thing to do to use Theorem 12.9.7 is to parameterize our surface and calculate \(\vw(t,s)=\vr_s \times \vr_t\) based on that parameterization.
In cylindrical coordinates, our cone is described by \(r=z\) which suggests that we can use \(r\) and \(\theta\) as \(s\) and \(t\text{.}\) Consider the parameterization \(\vr(s,t)=\langle s \cos(t), s \sin(t), s \rangle \) with bounds \(1\leq s \leq 3\) and \(0 \leq t \lt 2 \pi\text{.}\) Notice that \(s\) is acting as both \(r\) and \(z\text{.}\) Calculating the partial derivatives of \(\vr\) gives the following:
\begin{equation*} \vr_s = \langle \cos(t), \sin(t), 1 \rangle \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \vr_t = \langle -s \sin(t), s \cos(t), 0 \rangle \end{equation*}
Taking the cross product of \(\vr_s\) and \(\vr_t\) will give the following vector valued function for \(\vw\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \vw= \vr_s \times \vr_t = \langle -s \cos(t), -s \sin(t), s (\cos(t)^2 +\sin(t)^2) \rangle \end{equation*}
which will simplify to
\begin{equation*} \vw=\langle-s \cos(t), -s \sin(t), s \rangle \end{equation*}
This should look familiar-ish because \(\vw\) is very close to \(\vr\text{.}\) If we think of \(\vr\) as giving \(\langle x,y,z\rangle \text{,}\) then \(\vw = \langle -x, -y, z \rangle \text{.}\) The interact below shows the vectors \(\vr\) and \(\vw\) for a point on the surface we are examining.
In Figure 12.9.9 you can see that that the normal vector (as calculated from the parameterization) points inside of the cone and this will be the direction of positive flow when measuring the flux of the vector field. Our parameterization will also transform our vector field into a function of \(s\) and \(t\text{;}\) specifcally
\begin{equation*} \vF(s,t)= \langle (s \cos(t))(s) -2, s \sin(t) + s \cos(t) , 1\rangle \end{equation*}
Applying Theorem 12.9.7 to our parameterization and vector field gives the following iterated integral:
\begin{gather*} \iint_D \vF \cdot (\vr_s \times \vr_t)\, dA \\ \int_0^{2\pi}\int_1^3 \langle (s \cos(t))(s) -2, s \sin(t) + s \cos(t) , 1\rangle \cdot \langle -s \cos(t), -s \sin(t), s \rangle \enspace ds \enspace dt \\ \int_0^{2\pi}\int_1^3 ((s \cos(t))(s) -2) (-s \cos(t)) +(-s \sin(t)) (s \sin(t) + s \cos(t)) +s \\ -\frac{62}{3}\pi \end{gather*}
Note that the result of this flux integral is negative, which means more of the vector field is going out of the cone than is going into the cone.
In Figure 12.9.10 you can see much more of the vector field is going through to the outside of the cone than inside, which matches with our result of calculating the flux as negative.
