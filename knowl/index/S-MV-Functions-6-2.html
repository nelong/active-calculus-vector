Activity 9.1.3 showed that the equations where one independent variable is constant lead to planes parallel to ones that result from a pair of the coordinate axes. When we make the constant 0, we get the coordinate planes. The \(xy\)-plane satisfies \(z=0\text{,}\) the \(xz\)-plane satisfies \(y=0\text{,}\) and the \(yz\)-plane satisfies \(x=0\) (see Figure 11.1.10).
