If an oriented curve \(C\) ends at the same point where it started, we say that \(C\) is closed. The line integral of a vector field \(\vF\) along a closed curve \(C\) is called the circulation of \(\vF\) around \(C\text{.}\) To emphasize the fact that \(C\) is closed, we sometimes write \(\oint_C \vF\cdot d\vr\) for \(\int_C \vF\cdot d\vr\text{.}\) Circulation serves as a measure of a vector fieldâ€™s tendency to rotate in a manner consistent with the orientation of the (closed) curve and is measured by looking at whether the vector field is working with or against the motion along the path.
