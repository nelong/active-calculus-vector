We can evaluate the double integral \(\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA\) over a rectangle \(R = [a,b] \times [c,d]\) as an iterated integral in one of two ways:
  • -.
    \(\int_a^b \left( \int_c^d f(x,y) \, dy \right) \, dx\text{,}\) or
  • -.
    \(\int_c^d \left( \int_a^b f(x,y) \, dx \right) \, dy\text{.}\)
This process works because each inner integral represents a cross-sectional (signed) area and the outer integral then sums all of the cross-sectional (signed) areas. Fubiniâ€™s Theorem guarantees that the resulting value is the same, regardless of the order in which we integrate.
