Item.

If \(\vr\) is a vector-valued function, then the derivative of \(\vr\) is defined by
\begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\vr(t+h)-\vr(t)}{h} \end{equation*}
for those values of \(t\) at which the limit exists, and is computed componentwise by the formula
\begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = x'(t) \vi + y'(t) \vj + z'(t) \vk \end{equation*}
for those values of \(t\) at which \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) are differentiable, where \(\vr(t) = x(t) \vi + y(t) \vj + z(t) \vk\text{.}\)
