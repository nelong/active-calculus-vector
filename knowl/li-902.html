Item b.

There is no single second derivative of a function of two variables, so we consider a quantity that combines the second order partial derivatives. Let \(D = f_{xx}f_{yy} - f_{xy}^2\text{.}\) Calculate \(D\) at the origin for each of the functions \(f_1\text{,}\) \(f_2\text{,}\) and \(f_3\text{.}\) What difference do you notice between the values of \(D\) when a function has a maximum or minimum value at the origin versus when a function has a saddle point at the origin?
