In \(\R^3\text{,}\) the distance between points \(P=(x_0, y_0, z_0)\) and \(Q=(x_1, y_1, z_1)\) (denoted as \(|PQ|\)) is given by the formula

\begin{equation*} |PQ| = \sqrt{(x_1-x_0)^2 + (y_1-y_0)^2 + (z_1-z_0)^2}. \end{equation*}

and thus the equation of a sphere with center \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\) and radius \(r\) is

\begin{equation*} (x-x_0)^2 + (y-y_0)^2 + (z-z_0)^2 = r^2. \end{equation*}
