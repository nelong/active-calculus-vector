Recall that as \(n \to \infty\) we also have \(\Delta t \to 0\text{.}\) Since
\begin{align*} x'(t) \amp = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{x(t_i) - x(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t},\\ y'(t) \amp = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{y(t_i) - y(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}, \ \text{ and } \\ z'(t) \amp \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{z(t_i) - z(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}, \end{align*}
we see that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{\left(\frac{x(t_i) - x(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y(t_i) - y(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{z(t_i) - z(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}\right)^2} \Delta t \end{equation*}
is equal to
\begin{equation*} \int_a^b \sqrt{(x'(t))^2 + (y'(t))^2 + (z'(t))^2} \, dt. \end{equation*}
