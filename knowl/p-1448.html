Let's consider the function \(g\) defined by

\begin{equation*} g(x,y) = \frac{x^2y}{x^4 + y^2} \end{equation*}

and investigate the limit \(\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)}g(x,y)\text{.}\)

  1. What is the behavior of \(g\) on the \(x\)-axis? That is, what is \(g(x,0)\) and what is the limit of \(g\) as \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along the \(x\)-axis?

  2. What is the behavior of \(g\) on the \(y\)-axis? That is, what is \(g(0,y)\) and what is the limit of \(g\) as \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along the \(y\)-axis?

  3. What is the behavior of \(g\) on the line \(y=mx\text{?}\) That is, what is \(g(x,mx)\) and what is the limit of \(g\) as \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along the line \(y=mx\text{?}\)

  4. Based on what you have seen so far, do you think \(\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)}g(x,y)\) exists? If so, what do you think its value is?

  5. Now consider the behavior of \(g\) on the parabola \(y=x^2\text{?}\) What is \(g(x,x^2)\) and what is the limit of \(g\) as \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along this parabola?

  6. State whether the limit \(\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} g(x,y)\) exists or not and provide a justification of your statement.
