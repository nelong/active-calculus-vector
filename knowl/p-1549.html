Consider the function \(h\) defined by \(h(x,y) = \frac{2x^2y}{x^4 + y^2}.\)
  1. What is the domain of \(h\text{?}\)
  2. Evaluate the limit of \(h\) at \((0,0)\) along all linear paths the contain the origin. What does this tell us about \(\lim_{(x,y) \to (0,0)} h(x,y)\text{?}\) (Hint: A non-vertical line throught the origin has the form \(y = mx \) for some constant \(m\text{.}\))
  3. Does \(\lim_{(x,y) \to (0,0)} h(x,y)\) exist? Verify your answer. Check by using appropriate technology to sketch both surface and contour plots of \(h\) near \((0,0)\text{.}\) Write several sentences to say how your plots affirm your findings about \(\lim_{(x,y) \to (0,0)} h(x,y)\text{.}\)
