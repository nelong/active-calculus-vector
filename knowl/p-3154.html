A tetrahedron is a three-dimensional figure with four faces, each of which is a triangle. A picture of the tetrahedron \(T\) with vertices \((0,0,0)\text{,}\) \((1,0,0)\text{,}\) \((0,1,0)\text{,}\) and \((0,0,1)\) is shown at left in FigureÂ 11.3.1. If we place one vertex at the origin and let vectors \(\va\text{,}\) \(\vb\text{,}\) and \(\vc\) be determined by the edges of the tetrahedron that have one end at the origin, then a formula that tells us the volume \(V\) of the tetrahedron is
\begin{equation} V = \frac{1}{6} \lvert \va \cdot (\vb \times \vc) \rvert.\tag{11.3.1} \end{equation}
