Suppose that \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) are the vectors shown in Figure 9.2.8.

  1. On the axes at left in Figure 9.2.8, sketch the vectors \(\vu + \vv\text{,}\) \(\vv - \vu\text{,}\) \(2\vu\text{,}\) \(-2\vu\text{,}\) and \(-3\vv\text{.}\)

  2. What is \(0\vv\text{?}\)

  3. On the axes at right in Figure 9.2.8, sketch the vectors \(-3\vv\text{,}\) \(-2\vv\text{,}\) \(-1\vv\text{,}\) \(2\vv\text{,}\) and \(3\vv\text{.}\)

  4. Give a geometric description of the set of terminal points of the vectors \(t\vv\) where \(t\) is any scalar.

  5. On the set of axes at right in Figure 9.2.8, sketch the vectors \(\vu-3\vv\text{,}\) \(\vu-2\vv\text{,}\) \(\vu-\vv\text{,}\) \(\vu + \vv\text{,}\) and \(\vu + 2\vv\text{.}\)

  6. Give a geometric description of the set of terminal points of the vectors \(\vu + t\vv\) where \(t\) is any scalar.
