We now treat \(\delta(x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*) \Delta A\) as a point mass at the point \((x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*)\text{.}\) The coordinates \((\overline{x}, \overline{y})\) of the center of mass of these \(mn\) point masses are thus given by
\begin{equation*} \overline{x} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{i=1}^m x_{ij}^*\delta(x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*) \Delta A}{\sum_{j=1}^n\sum_{i=1}^m \delta(x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*) \Delta A} \ \ \ \ \ \text{ and } \ \ \ \ \ \overline{y} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{i=1}^m y_{ij}^*\delta(x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*) \Delta A}{\sum_{j=1}^n\sum_{i=1}^m \delta(x_{ij}^*,y_{ij}^*) \Delta A}. \end{equation*}
