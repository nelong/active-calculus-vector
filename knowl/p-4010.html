Assume the density of \(S\) is given by \(\delta(x,y,z) = z\)
  1. Set up (but do not evaluate) an iterated integral that represents the mass of \(S\text{.}\) Integrate first with respect to \(z\text{,}\) then \(y\text{,}\) then \(x\text{.}\) A picture of the projection of \(S\) onto the \(xy\)-plane is shown at left in Figure 11.7.9.
  2. Set up (but do not evaluate) an iterated integral that represents the mass of \(S\text{.}\) In this case, integrate first with respect to \(y\text{,}\) then \(z\text{,}\) then \(x\text{.}\) A picture of the projection of \(S\) onto the \(xz\)-plane is shown at center in Figure 11.7.9.
  3. Set up (but do not evaluate) an iterated integral that represents the mass of \(S\text{.}\) For this integral, integrate first with respect to \(x\text{,}\) then \(y\text{,}\) then \(z\text{.}\) A picture of the projection of \(S\) onto the \(yz\)-plane is shown at right in Figure 11.7.9.
  4. Which of these three orders of integration is the most natural to you? Why?
