ActivityÂ 11.9.2 presents the general idea of how a change of variables works. We partition a rectangular domain in the \(st\) system into subrectangles. Let \(T = [a, b] \times [a+\Delta s, b+\Delta t]\) be one of these subrectangles. Then we transform this into a region \(T'\) in the standard \(xy\) Cartesian coordinate system. The region \(T'\) is called the image of \(T\text{;}\) the region \(T\) is the pre-image of \(T'\text{.}\) Although the sides of this \(xy\) region \(T'\) aren't necessarily straight (linear), we will approximate the element of area \(dA\) for this region with the area of the parallelogram whose sides are given by the vectors \(\vv\) and \(\vw\text{,}\) where \(\vv\) is the vector from \((x(a, b), y(a, b))\) to \((x(a + \Delta s, b), y(a + \Delta s, b))\text{,}\) and \(\vw\) is the vector from \((x(a, b), y(a, b))\) to \((x(a, b + \Delta t), y(a, b + \Delta t))\text{.}\)
