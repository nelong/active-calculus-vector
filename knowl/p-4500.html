Just as when we differentiated a vector-valued function \(\vr(t)\) to find a tangent vector, we begin by dividing a curve \(C\) oriented from a point \(P\) to a point \(Q\) into \(n\) small, straight pieces. Each of these pieces is in an area where the vector field \(\vF\) is nearly constant, provided we use enough pieces. In FigureÂ 12.2.2, we show this situation. Each \(\vr_i\) is the tip of a vector that traces out the curve, which makes the vectors \(\Delta\vr_i = \vr_{i+1}-\vr_i\) (shown in blue) approximate the curve \(C\text{.}\) The green vectors are the vectors in the vector field \(\vF\) at each of the designated points along the curve.
