Suppose that \(C\) is an oriented curve traced out by the vector-valued function \(\vr(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) and let \(\vF\) be a cnotinuous vector field defined on a region cnotaining \(C\text{.}\) We can divide the interval \([a,b]\) into \(n\) sub-intervals, each of length \(\Delta t = (b-a)/n\text{,}\) by letting \(t_i = a + i\Delta t\) for \(i = 0,1,\dots,n\text{.}\) This subdivision of \([a,b]\) then can be used to break \(C\) up into \(n\) pieces by letting \(\Delta \vr_i = \vr(t_{i+1}) - \vr(t_{i})\) for \(i=0,1,\dots,n-1\) as we did in FigureÂ 12.2.2. Now notice that

\begin{equation*} \Delta \vr_i = \vr(t_{i+1}) - \vr(t_{i}) = \vr(t_{i} + \Delta t) - \vr(t_{i}) = \left(\frac{\vr(t_{i} + \Delta t) - \vr(t_{i})}{\Delta t}\right) \Delta t \text{.} \end{equation*}
