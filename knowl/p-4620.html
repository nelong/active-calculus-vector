It is important to recognize that the integral on the right-hand side is still a line integral and must be evaluated using techniques for evaluating line integrals. We cannot simply try to treat the line integral (of the form \(\int_C F_1\, dx + F_2\, dy + F_3\, dz\)) as if it were a definite integral of a function of one variable. Because the notation \(\int_C\vF\cdot d\vr\) provides a reminder that this is a line integral and not a definite integral of the types calculated earlier in your study of calculus, we will only use the vector notation for line integrals in the body of the text. However, some exercises may require the use of the differential form.
