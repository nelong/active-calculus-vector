Let \(C_1\) be part of the unit circle on the \(xy\)-plane that is centered at the origin and is above the line given by \(y=-x\text{.}\) We will also consider \(g(x,y,z)=x-y\text{.}\) We can parameterize \(C_1\) by \(r(t)=\langle \cos(t), \sin(t), 0 \rangle\) with \(t \in [-\frac{\pi}{4},\frac{3\pi}{4}]\text{.}\) Note here that \(\Vert{\frac{dr}{dt}}\Vert =\Vert{\langle -\sin(t),\cos(t),0\rangle}\Vert=1\text{.}\) We can rewrite the output of \(g\) along \(C_1\) using the parameterization as \(g(x(t),y(t),z(t))=g(\cos(t),\sin(t),0)=\cos(t)-\sin(t)\text{.}\) Applying TheoremÂ 12.4.5, we get
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_1} g \enspace ds = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{4}}^{\frac{3\pi}{4}} \cos(t)-\sin(t) (1) dt = \sin(\frac{3\pi}{4})-\cos(\frac{3\pi}{4}) - [\sin(-\frac{\pi}{4})-\cos(-\frac{\pi}{4}))]=0 \end{equation*}
