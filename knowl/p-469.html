Let \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vw\) be vectors in \(\R^n\text{.}\) Then
  1. \(\vu \cdot \vv = \vv \cdot \vu\) (the dot product is commutative), and
  2. \((\vu + \vv) \cdot \vw = (\vu \cdot \vw) + (\vv \cdot \vw)\text{.}\)
  3. if \(c\) is a scalar, then \((c\vu) \cdot \vw = c(\vu \cdot \vw)\text{.}\)
