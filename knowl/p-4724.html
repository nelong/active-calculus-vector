Let's look at Definition 12.4.5 again where we have a parameterization for \(C\) given by a vector valued function of one variable \(r(t)=\langle f(t), g(t), h(t)\rangle\) for \(t\) in some interval \([a,b]\text{.}\) Instead of thinking in terms of pieces of the curve \(C\text{,}\) the parameterization allows us to break the interval \([a,b]\) into pieces \(a=t_0, t_1, ... ,t_k=b\) where \(t_i=a+i(\Delta{t})\) and \(\Delta{t}=\frac{b-a}{k}\text{.}\) While these pieces will be equally spaced in terms of the parameter \(t\text{,}\) the corresponding points on the curve \(C\) given by \(r(t_i)\) will not be equally spaced. We can simplify the notation of our function evaluation as follows:

\begin{equation*} f(r_i^{*})=f(r(t_i^{*}))=f(t_i^{*}) \end{equation*}

where \(t_i^{*} \in [t_i,t_{i+1}]\text{.}\) Remember that the parameterization will allow us to write all parts of the scalar line integral as a function of \(t\text{,}\) so we will simplify \(\Vert{r_{i+1}-r_i}\Vert\) as follows:

\begin{equation*} \Vert{r_{i+1}-r_i}\Vert =\Vert{r(t_{i+1})-r(t_i)}\Vert= \frac{\Vert{r(t_{i+1})-r(t_i)}\Vert}{\Delta{t}}\Delta{t} \end{equation*}

Applying our parameterization and corresponding points to Definition Definition 12.4.5 will give the following

\begin{equation*} \int_C f \enspace ds =\lim_{k\rightarrow \infty} \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} f(r_i^{*}) \Vert{r_{i+1}-r_{i}}\Vert=\lim_{k\rightarrow \infty} \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} f(t_i^{*}) \frac{\Vert{r(t_{i+1})-r(t_i)}\Vert}{\Delta{t}}\Delta{t} \end{equation*}

This is the Riemann Sum of a scalar function of \(t\text{,}\) specifically \(f(t) v(t)\) where \(v(t)=\frac{d\Vert{r}\Vert}{dt}\) (\(v(t)\) is the speed of the parameterization \(r\)).
