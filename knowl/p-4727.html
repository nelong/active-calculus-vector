If \(\vr(t) = \langle x(t), y(t), z(t)\rangle\text{,}\) then the integrand above is
\begin{align*} \amp \phantom{=} \langle f_x(\vr(t)), f_y(\vr(t)), f_z(\vr(t))\rangle\cdot \langle x'(t),y'(t),z'(t)\rangle \\ \amp= f_x\left( x(t),y(t),z(t) \right) \, x'(t) + f_y \left( x(t),y(t),z(t) \right) \, y'(t) \\ \amp\phantom{=} + f_z \left( x(t),y(t),z(t) \right) \, z'(t)\text{.} \end{align*}
Or more simply,
\begin{equation*} \nabla f \cdot \frac{d \vr}{dt}= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{dx}{dt}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \frac{dy}{dt}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial z} \frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}\left(f(\vr(t))\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
