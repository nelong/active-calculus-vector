This result is so important that it is frequently called the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus for Line Integrals, because of its similarity to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus 2 , which can be written as
\begin{equation*} \int_a^b \frac{df}{dx} (x)\, dx = f(b) - f(a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
