We can choose \(h(x,y,z)=xe^{z^2}+x^2y^3z+\sin(x)+y^3\cos(x)+e^{y^2}-z^4\) and note that \(\nabla h = \vH\text{.}\) so by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus for Line Integrals, \(\int_C \vH\cdot d\vr = h(0,0,0)-h(1,1,1)=1-(e+1+\sin(1)+\cos(1)+e-1)=1-(2e+\sin(1)+\cos(1))\text{.}\)
