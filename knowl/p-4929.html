We have shown that \(\int_C f \enspace ds = int_{C_3} f \enspace ds \) and want to demonstrate why \(int_{C_3} f \enspace ds \) will be positive. Because the output of \(f\) is positive for all of the points on the curve \(C_3\text{,}\) all elements of the associated Riemann sum in DefinitionÂ 12.5.5 will be positive (the length of the displacement vectors and corresponding arc lengths are always positive). Thus \(\int_C f \enspace ds = int_{C_3} f \enspace ds \gt 0\text{.}\) This argument would work well for any curve that was only in the first and second octants because the outpuf of \(g\) for all of those points would be negative.
