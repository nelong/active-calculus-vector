We can simplify the notation of our function evaluation as follows:
\begin{equation*} f(r_i^{*})=f(r(t_i^{*}))=f(t_i^{*}) \end{equation*}
where \(t_i^{*} \in [t_i,t_{i+1}]\text{.}\) Remember that the parameterization will allow us to write all parts of the scalar line integral as a function of \(t\text{,}\) so we will simplify \(\Vert{r_{i+1}-r_i}\Vert\) as follows:
\begin{equation*} \Vert{r_{i}-r_{i-1}}\Vert =\Vert{r(t_{i})-r(t_{i-1})}\Vert= \frac{\Vert{r(t_{i})-r(t_{i-1})}\Vert}{\Delta{t}}\Delta{t} \end{equation*}
Applying our parameterization and corresponding points to Definition DefinitionÂ 12.5.5 will give the following
\begin{equation*} \int_C f \enspace ds =\lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(r_i^{*}) \Vert{r_{i}-r_{i-1}}\Vert=\lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(t_i^{*}) \frac{\Vert{r(t_{i-1}+\Delta t)-r(t_{i-1})}\Vert}{\Delta{t}}\Delta{t} \end{equation*}
This is the Riemann Sum of a scalar function of \(t\text{,}\) specifically \(f(t) v(t)\) where \(v(t)=\frac{d\Vert{r}\Vert}{dt}\) (\(v(t)\) is the speed of the parameterization \(r\)).
