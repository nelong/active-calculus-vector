Let’s look at applying a parameterization for \(C\) given by a vector-valued function of one variable \(\vr(t)=\langle f(t), g(t), h(t)\rangle\) for \(t\) in some interval \([a,b]\) to Definition 12.5.5. Instead of thinking in terms of pieces of the curve \(C\text{,}\) the parameterization allows us to break the interval \([a,b]\) into pieces \(a=t_0, t_1, \dots ,t_n=b\) where \(t_i=a+i(\Delta{t})\) and \(\Delta{t}=\frac{b-a}{n}\text{.}\) While these pieces will be equally spaced in terms of the parameter \(t\text{,}\) the corresponding points on the curve \(C\) given by \(r(t_i)\) may not be equally spaced.
