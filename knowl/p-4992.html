We will compute our circulation density as

\begin{equation*} \text{Circulation Density}=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \frac{\text{Total Circulation}}{\text{Area of Square}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since the area of the square is \((2h)^2 = 4h^2\text{,}\) this gives us that the circulation density is

\begin{equation*} \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \frac{F_2(a+h,b_{\text{right}}^*)-F_2(a-h,b_{\text{left}}^*)}{2h} - \frac{F_1(a_{\text{top}}^*,b+h)-F_1(a_{\text{bottom}}^*,b-h)}{2h}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Recall the central difference method of estimating derivatives from Section 1.5.2 1  and notice that as \(h\to 0\text{,}\) \(a_{\text{top}}^*,a_{\text{bottom}}^*\) must go to \(a\) and \(b_{\text{left}}^*,b_{\text{right}}^*\) must go to \(b\text{.}\) Therefore, our circulation density measurement at a point is

\begin{equation*} \text{Circulation Density at the point }(a,b)= \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} (a,b)-\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y} (a,b) \end{equation*}
