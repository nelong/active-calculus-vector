In this section, we will go through the details for how to measure the density of the “creation” or “destruction” of the vector field in a classic calculus fashion. Specifically, we will measure how the strength of the vector field changes in a region around a point. Next, using a limit, we examine what happens to our measurement as we shrink the region. Because vector fields change in a continuous fashion, the vector fields don’t actually change at a single point. Rather, we will measure the density for the change in strength of the vector field.
