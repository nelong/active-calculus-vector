In FigureÂ 12.9.1, you can see a surface plotted using a parametrization \(\vr(s,t)=\langle{f(s,t),g(s,t),h(s,t)}\rangle\text{.}\) The red lines represent curves where \(s\) varies and \(t\) is held constant, while the yellow lines represent curves where \(t\) varies and \(s\) is held constant. The vector in red is \(\vr_s=\frac{\partial \vr}{\partial s}=\langle{f_s,g_s,h_s}\rangle\) which measures the direction and magnitude of change in the coordinates of the surface when just \(s\) is varied. Similarly, the vector in yellow is \(\vr_t=\frac{\partial \vr}{\partial t}=\langle{f_t,g_t,h_t}\rangle\) which measures the direction and magnitude of change in the coordinates of the surface when just \(t\) is varied. You can see that the parallelogram that is formed by \(\vr_s\) and \(\vr_t\) is tangent to the surface. The area of this parallelogram offers an approximation for the surface area of a patch of the surface.
