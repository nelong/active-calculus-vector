As with our development of divergence, we apply the Mean Value Theorem to the four integrals above to simplify future calculations. Specifically, the Mean Value Theorem applied to the first integral tells us that there is a value, \(a_{\text{top}}^*\text{,}\) in the interval \((a-h,a+h)\) such that
\begin{equation*} \int_{-h}^h -F_1( a-t,b+h) \, dt= -2h \left(F_1(a_{\text{top}}^*,b+h)\right) \end{equation*}
Applying the same arguments to the other three integrals in our Total Circulation calculation gives
\begin{align*} \text{Total Circulation} =\amp \int_{\text{Square}} \vF \cdot d\vr\\ =\amp \int_{-h}^h -F_1(a-t,b+h) \, dt + \int_{-h}^h -F_2(a-h,b-t) \, dt\\ \amp + \int_{-h}^h F_1(a+t,b-h) \, dt + \int_{-h}^h F_2(a+h,b+t) \, dt\\ =\amp -2h F_1(a_{\text{top}}^*,b+h) -2h F_2(a-h,b_{\text{left}}^*) \\ \amp + 2h F_1(a_{\text{bottom}}^*,b-h)+2h F_2(a+h,b_{\text{right}}^*) \end{align*}
Here we have \(a_{\text{top}}^*,a_{\text{bottom}}^* \in (a-h,a+h)\) and \(b_{\text{left}}^*,b_{\text{right}}^* \in (b-h,b+h)\text{.}\) We can regroup these terms to get the following expression for the total circulation around our square:
\begin{gather*} 2h\left(F_2(a+h,b_{\text{right}}^*)-F_2(a-h,b_{\text{left}}^*)\right) \\ \quad -2h\left(F_1(a_{\text{top}}^*,b+h)-F_1(a_{\text{bottom}}^*,b-h)\right) \end{gather*}
