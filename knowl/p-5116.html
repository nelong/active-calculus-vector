In this example, we will look at \(\vG=\langle x,y,z\rangle \text{.}\) So by Subsection 12.7.3,
\begin{equation*} \curl(\vG)= \frac{\langle \partial}{\partial y}(z) -\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(y), - \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(z)-\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(x) \right) , \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (y)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x) \rangle \end{equation*}
So \(\curl(\vF)=\langle 0,0,0\rangle\text{.}\) Thus, \(\vG\) will have no rotational strength anywhere. If you look at the plot of \(\vG\) in Figure 12.7.12, then you will see that dropping a spinner at any point in space will not have the spinner rotate. No matter what orientation the spinner will have, there will be equal force on each side of the spinner, thus the spinner will not rotate.
