With these conventions, the output vector of the curl evaluated at a point \(P\text{,}\) written as \(\curl(\vF)(P)\text{,}\) will have the following properties:
  1. The direction of \(\curl(\vF)(P)\) will be the axis of rotation that has the strongest positive rotational strength.
  2. The magnitude of \(\curl(\vF)(P)\) will be the circulation density of the vector field in the plane through \(P\) with normal vector \(\curl(\vF)(P)\text{.}\)
