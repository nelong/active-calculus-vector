In order to create a Riemann sum that will measure the acculumation of \(f(x,y,z)\text{,}\) a scalar valued function, over \(S_1\text{,}\) a smooth, bounded surface in space, we will break our surface into smaller piecese where we can approximate the output of \(f\) and the surface area of each piece. In Subsection 11.6.2 and Section 12.9, we developed the way to measure surface area given a parameterization for the surface.
