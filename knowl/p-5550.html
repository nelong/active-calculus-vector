In our case, there is more surface area of \(S_1\) inside the parabolic cylinder than outside. For \((x,y,z)\) points that are far away from the yellow surface, the magnitude of the output of \(g\) for that point will be large. For \((x,y,z)\) points that are close to the yellow surface, thier \(z\)-coordinates will be close to \(x^2\) and thus the output of \(g\) will be close to zero. This means that in order to properly estimate the scalar surface integral, we will need to assess whether there is a greater weighted surface area inside the parabolic cylinder than outside, where the weight of each surface area piece is given by how far the piece is from the yellow surface (the parabolic cylinder surface). This is not an easy argument to make and is not easy to verify visually for this example.
