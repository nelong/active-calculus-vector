Before stating some useful properties of scalar line integrals, we will recall some convenient notation. If \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) are disjoint surfaces 1 , we denote by \(S_1+S_2\) the surface containing every point that is \(S_1\) or \(S_2\text{.}\) Also, if \(S_1\) is a surface, then \(-S_1\) denotes the same surface but parameterized in such a way that the normal vector points in the opposite direction.
