We can now use Theorem 12.10.4 to compute the scalar surface integral as
\begin{align*} \iint_{S_1} x \, dS \amp = \int_0^{\pi/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \left[\cos(s)\sin(t)\right] (\sin(t)) \, ds\, dt\\ \amp = \left[\sin(s)\right]_{0}^{2\pi}\int_0^{\pi}(\sin(t))^2\, dt = 0\cdot \int_0^{\pi}(\sin(t))^2\, dt =0\text{.} \end{align*}
This result should match your answer for part a of Activity 12.10.2 since the values of the function \(f(x,y,z)=x\) are symmetric but opposite in sign for the parts of the surface in the octants with \(x\gt 0\) as compared to the octants with \(x\lt 0\text{.}\)
