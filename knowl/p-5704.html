In this task, we will calculate the left side of the Divergence Theorem using four flux integrals (for each of the four boundary surfaces) as drawn in FigureÂ 12.12.9. We will need to parametrize each of the four faces which we will call \(S_1\) (in red), \(S_2\) (in yellow), \(S_3\) (in blue), and \(S_4\) (in green).
  • \(\displaystyle \vr_1(s,t)= \langle 0,s,t \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vr_2(s,t)= \langle s,0,t \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vr_3(s,t)= \langle s,t,0 \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vr_4(s,t)= \langle s,t,1-s-t \rangle\)
where all of these parameterization use \(0\leq s \leq 1\) and \(0 \leq t \leq 1-s\text{.}\) Each of these parameterizations gives the following normal vectors:
  • \(\displaystyle \vn_1 = \langle 1,0,0 \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vn_2 = \langle 0,-1,0 \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vn_3 = \langle 0,0,1 \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \vn_4 = \langle 1,1,1 \rangle\)
Notice that \(\vn_1\) and \(\vn_3\) point into \(Q\) while \(\vn_2\) and \(\vn_4\) points out of \(Q\text{.}\) We will take into account the idea that we will need to calculate the flow out of \(Q\) when we sum our flux integrals later.
