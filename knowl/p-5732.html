Use Stokes' Theorem to evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_{C} \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}\) where \(\mathbf{F}(x, y, z) = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + 9\!(x^{2} + y^{2})\mathbf{k}\) and \(C\) is the boundary of the part of the paraboloid where \(z = 1 - x^{2} - y^{2}\) which lies above the xy-plane and \(C\) is oriented counterclockwise when viewed from above.
