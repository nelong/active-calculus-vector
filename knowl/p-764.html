The fixed vector \(\vv\) in the definition is called a direction vector for the line. As we saw in Preview ActivityÂ 9.5.1, to find an equation for a line through point \(P\) in the direction of vector \(\vv\text{,}\) observe that any vector parallel to \(\vv\) will have the form \(t \vv\) for some scalar \(t\text{.}\) So, any vector emanating from the point \(P\) in a direction parallel to the vector \(\vv\) will be of the form
\begin{equation} \overrightarrow{OP} + \vv t\tag{9.5.1} \end{equation}
for some scalar \(t\) (where \(O\) is the origin).
