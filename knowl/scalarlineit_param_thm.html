Theorem 12.4.5. Calculating Scalar Line Integrals with Parameterizations.

Let \(f(x,y,z)\) be a continuous multivariable function around a curve in space \(C\text{,}\) where \(C\) has a parameterization given by \(r(t)\) for \(t \in [a,b]\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \int_C f \enspace ds =\int_a^b f(r(t)) \Vert{\frac{dr}{dt}}\Vert \enspace dt \end{equation*}
