(a) The displacement from \(P\) to \(Q\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \overrightarrow{PQ} = (5\,\vec i + 6\,\vec j) - (1\,\vec i + 3\,\vec j) = 4\,\mathit{\vec i}+3\,\mathit{\vec j}. \end{equation*}
Since
\begin{equation*} \mbox{len}{\overrightarrow{PQ}} = \sqrt{4^2 + 3^2} = 5, \end{equation*}
a unit vector \(\vec u\) in the direction of \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec u = \frac{1}{5}\,\overrightarrow{PQ} = \frac{1}{5}\,(4\,\mathit{\vec i}+3\,\mathit{\vec j}) . \end{equation*}
(b) A vector of length 15 pointing in the same direction is given by
\begin{equation*} 15\,\vec u = \frac{15}{5}\,(4\,\mathit{\vec i}+3\,\mathit{\vec j}) = 12\,\mathit{\vec i}+9\,\mathit{\vec j}. \end{equation*}
