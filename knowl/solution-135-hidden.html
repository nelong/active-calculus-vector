Let \(C_1\) be given by \(\vr(t) =\langle 2\cos(-t),2\sin(-t)\rangle\) where \(-\pi \leq t\leq 0\text{.}\) This means that \(\vF(t) = \langle 0, 2\cos(-t) \rangle\) and
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_1} \vF\cdot d\vr =-4\int_{-\pi}^0 (\cos(t))^2 \enspace dt = -2 \pi\text{.} \end{equation*}
Let \(C_2\) be given by \(\vr(t) =\langle t,0\rangle\) where \(-2 \leq t\leq 2\text{.}\) This means that \(\vF(t) = \langle 0, t \rangle\) and
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_2} \vF\cdot d\vr =\int_{-2}^2 0 \enspace dt = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus the line integral over \(C_1\) then \(C_2\) will give a result of \(-2\pi\text{.}\)
