Solution 12.3.5.4.1.

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) = \left\lt -2 \sin (- t^3), -2 \cos (- t^2), 3 t^4 \right> \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{r} '(t) = \left\lt -3 t^2, -2 t, -3 \right > \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) \cdot \mathbf{r} '(t) = \left\lt -2 \sin (- t^3), -2 \cos (- t^2), 3 t^4 \right> \cdot \left\lt -3 t^2, -2 t, -3 \right > \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =6 t^2 \sin (- t^3) + 4 t \cos (- t^2) - 9 t^4 \end{equation*}
The line integral is then:
\begin{equation*} \int_{\mathcal{C}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \,d\ \mathbf{r} = \int^1_0 \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) \cdot \mathbf{r} '(t) \ dt = \int^1_0 6 t^2 \sin (- t^3) + 4 t \cos (- t^2) - 9 t^4\ dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \left [ 2 \cos (- t^3) - 2 \sin (- t^2) + \frac{-9}{5} t^5 \right ]^1_0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 2 \cos (-1) - 2 \sin (-1) + \frac{-19}{5} \end{equation*}
