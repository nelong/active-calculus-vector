The line segment \(C_1\) can be given by \(\vr(t) = \langle t,t\rangle\) as \(0\leq t\leq 2\rangle\text{,}\) which means that \(\vF \cdot d\vr=\langle t^2,t^2\rangle \cdot \langle 1,1\rangle \enspace dt=2t^2 dt\text{.}\) Thus,
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_1} \vF \cdot d\vr=\int_0^2 2t^2 \enspace dt = \frac{16}{3} \end{equation*}
