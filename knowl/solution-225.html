Solution 12.8.5.3.1.

SOLUTION
Let \(D\) be the region enclosed by the curve \(C\text{.}\)
First note that the curve has a positive orientation. We will use Green’s Theorem. We have \(P = 4 x - 3 y, \; Q = 5 x\text{,}\) therefore
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} =5+ 3 = 8 . \end{equation*}
Hence, Green’s Theorem implies
\(\begin{array}{lcl} \int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\amp = \amp \iint_D \left( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \right) dA \\ \amp =\amp \iint_D 8 dA \\ \amp = \amp 8 \int_0^{\pi/3} \int_0^{2} r \, dr \, d\theta \quad \text{[Using polar coordinates]} \\\amp = \amp 8 \left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right) \left(\frac{ 2^2}{2}\right) \\\amp =\amp {\frac{16}{3}} \pi \end{array}\)
