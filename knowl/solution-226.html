(a) We use Greenâ€™s Theorem. Let \(R\) be the region enclosed by the circle \(C\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_R\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(7 e^{y^2} + 27 x) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(3 x^2 y + y^3 + 3 e^x)\right)\,dA = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int_R 27 - 3(x^2 + y^2)\, dA. \end{equation*}
Converting to polar coordinates, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1 (27 - 3 r^2)r\,dr\,d\theta = 2\pi\left(\frac{27}{2} r^2 - \frac{3}{4} r^4\right)\bigg|_0^1 = \frac{51}{2}\pi. \end{equation*}
(b) The integrand of the integral over the disk \(R\) is \(27 - 3(x^2+y^2)\text{.}\) Since the integrand is positive for \(x^2 + y^2 \lt \frac{27}{3} = 9\) and negative for \(x^2 + y^2 > 9\text{,}\) the integrand is positive inside the circle of radius 3 and negative outside that circle. Thus, the integral over \(R\) increases with \(a\) until \(a=3\) and then decreases. The maximum value of the line integral occurs when \(a = 3\text{.}\)
