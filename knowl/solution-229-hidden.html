(a) The curve, \(C\text{,}\) is closed and oriented in the correct direction for Green's Theorem. Writing \(A\) for the interior of the circle, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_C \left( 3(x^2-y)\vec i +7(y^2+x)\vec j\right)\cdot d\vec r = \int_A \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial x}7 (y^2+x) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}3(x^2-y)\right) dx\,dy \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_A (7-(-3))\, dx\,dy = 10 \int_A dx\,dy = 10\cdot \mbox{Area of }A = 250\pi. \end{equation*}
(b) The circle given has radius \(R\) and center \((a,b)\text{.}\) The argument in part (a) works for any circle of radius \(R\text{,}\) oriented counterclockwise. So the line integral has the value \(10\pi R^2\text{.}\)
