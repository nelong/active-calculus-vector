Let \(D\) be the region enclosed by the curve \(C\text{.}\)
First note that the curve has a positive orientation. We will use Green's Theorem. We have \(P = 3 x - 2 y, \; Q = 4 x\text{,}\) therefore
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} =4+ 2 = 6 . \end{equation*}
Hence, Green's Theorem implies
\(\begin{array}{lcl} \int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\amp = \amp \iint_D \left( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \right) dA \\ \amp =\amp \iint_D 6 dA \\ \amp = \amp 6 \int_0^{\pi/3} \int_0^{3} r \, dr \, d\theta \quad \text{[Using polar coordinates]} \\\amp = \amp 6 \left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right) \left(\frac{ 3^2}{2}\right) \\\amp =\amp 9 \pi \end{array}\)
