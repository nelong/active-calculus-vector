Solution 12.9.5.1.1.

(a) We use Green's Theorem. Let \(R\) be the region enclosed by the circle \(C\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_R\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(3 e^{y^2} + 225 x) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(9 x^2 y + 3 y^3 + 2 e^x)\right)\,dA = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int_R 225 - 9(x^2 + y^2)\, dA. \end{equation*}
Converting to polar coordinates, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1 (225 - 9 r^2)r\,dr\,d\theta = 2\pi\left(\frac{225}{2} r^2 - \frac{9}{4} r^4\right)\bigg|_0^1 = \frac{441}{2}\pi. \end{equation*}
(b) The integrand of the integral over the disk \(R\) is \(225 - 9(x^2+y^2)\text{.}\) Since the integrand is positive for \(x^2 + y^2 \lt \frac{225}{9} = 25\) and negative for \(x^2 + y^2 > 25\text{,}\) the integrand is positive inside the circle of radius 5 and negative outside that circle. Thus, the integral over \(R\) increases with \(a\) until \(a=5\) and then decreases. The maximum value of the line integral occurs when \(a = 5\text{.}\)
