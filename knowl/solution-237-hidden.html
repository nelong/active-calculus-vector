\(S\) is parameterized by
\begin{equation*} \vec r(r,\theta)=r\cos \theta\vec i+r\sin \theta\vec j+r\vec k, \end{equation*}
with \(0\le r\le R\) and \(0\le\theta\le 2\pi\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial r}\times \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial \theta} = \left|\begin{array}{ccc} \vec i \amp \vec j \amp \vec k\\ \cos{\theta} \amp \sin{\theta} \amp 1 \\ -r\sin{\theta} \amp r\cos{\theta} \amp 0 \end{array}\right| = - r \cos\theta \vec i - r \sin \theta \vec j + r \vec k. \end{equation*}
Since the vector \(\partial \vec r / \partial r \times \partial \vec r / \partial \theta\) points upward, in the direction opposite to the specified orientation, we use
\begin{equation*} d\vec A= -\left(\partial\vec r/\partial r\times\partial\vec r/\partial\theta\right) \,dr\,d\theta = (r\cos\theta\vec i + r\sin\theta\vec j - r\vec k)\, dr\,d\theta\,. \end{equation*}
Hence
\begin{equation*} \int_S\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^R 2 (r^5 \cos^2\theta\sin^2\theta\vec k)\cdot (r \cos\theta \vec i + r \sin \theta \vec j - r \vec k)\,dr\,d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^{R} -2 r^6\cos^2{\theta}\sin^2{\theta}\,dr\,d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = -\frac{2 R^7}{7}\int_0^{2\pi} \sin^2{\theta}\cos^2{\theta}\, d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = -\frac{2 R^7}{7}\int_0^{2\pi} \sin^2{\theta}(1-\sin^2{\theta})\, d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = -\frac{ 2 R^7}{7}\int_0^{2\pi} (\sin^2{\theta}-\sin^4{\theta})\, d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = -\left(\frac{2 R^7}{7}\right)\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)= -\frac{1}{14} \pi R^7. \end{equation*}
The cone is not differentiable at the point \((0,0)\text{.}\) However the flux integral, which is improper, converges.
