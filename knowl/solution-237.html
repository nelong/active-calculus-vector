Solution 12.9.4.8.1.

\(y=g( x,z )\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f( x,y,z ) \,dS= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} f\left(x,g( x,z ),z\right)\sqrt{1+ g_x^2+ g_z^2} \,dx \,dz \quad \mathbf{(1)} \end{equation*}
Since \(y=g( x,z )=5- z^2\text{,}\) we have \(g_x =0\text{,}\) \(g_z =-2z\text{,}\) hence:
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{1+ g_x^2+ g_z^2}=\sqrt{1+4 z^2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f\left(x,g( x,z ),z\right)=z \end{equation*}
The domain of integration is the square \(\left[0,8\right]\times \left[0,8\right]\) in the \(xz\)-plane. By \(\mathbf{(1)}\) we get:
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f( x,y,z ) \,dS = \int_0^{8} \int_0^{8} z\sqrt{1+4 z^2} \,dz \,dx= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left(\int_0^{8} 1 \,dx\right)\left(\int_0^{8} z \sqrt{1+4 z^2} \,dz\right)=8\int_0^{8} z \sqrt{1+4 z^2} \,dz \end{equation*}
We use the substitution \(u={1+4 z^2}, \,du= 8z \,dz\) to compute the integral. This gives:
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f( x,y,z ) \,dS = 8\int_0^{8} z\sqrt{1+4 z^2} \,dz= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 8\int_1^{257} \frac{u^{1/2}}{8} \,du = \frac{16}3\cdot\left(\frac{{257}^{3/2}-1}{8}\right)\approx 2746.02 \end{equation*}
