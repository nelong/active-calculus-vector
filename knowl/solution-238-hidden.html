\(\partial \vec r / \partial s \times \partial \vec r / \partial t\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial s}\times\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial t}= \left|\begin{array}{ccc} \quad\vec i\quad\amp \quad\vec j\quad\amp \quad\vec k\quad\\ 2s \amp 2 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 2t \amp 5 \end{array} \right| = 10\vec i- 10 s\vec j+4st\vec k. \end{equation*}
Since the \(z\)-component, \(4st\text{,}\) of the vector \(\partial \vec r / \partial s \times \partial \vec r / \partial t\) is positive for \(0 \lt s \leq 3, 2 \leq t \leq 4\text{,}\) we see that \(\partial \vec r/\partial s \times \partial \vec r / \partial t\) points upward, in the direction of the orientation of \(S\) we were given. Thus, we use
\begin{equation*} d\vec A = \left(\frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial s} \times \frac{\partial \vec r}{\partial t}\right)\,ds\,dt, \end{equation*}
and so we have
\begin{equation*} \int_S\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_0^{3}\int_{2}^{4} (5 t\vec i + 2 s^2\vec j)\cdot (10\vec i- 10 s\vec j+4st\vec k)\,dt\,ds \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\int_0^{3}\int_{2}^{4} (50 t - 20 s^3)\,dt\,ds = \int_0^{3}(25 t^2 - 20 s^3 t)\bigg|_{t=2}^{t=4}\,ds \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\int_0^{3}(300 - 40 s^3)\,ds=(300s - 10 s^4)\bigg|_0^{3} =90. \end{equation*}
