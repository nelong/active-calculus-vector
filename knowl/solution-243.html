Solution 12.10.4.2.1.

\(x=3 u+6\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=5 u+v\) satisfy the equation of the plane,
\begin{equation*} 2x-y-z =2(3 u+6 )-( u-v )-( 5 u+v )= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 6 u+12-u+v-5 u-v=12 \end{equation*}
Moreover, for any \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\) satisfying \(2x-y-z=12\text{,}\) there are values of \(u\) and \(v\) such that \(x=3 u+6\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=5 u+v\text{,}\) since the following equations can be solved for \(u\) and \(v\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl}x\amp {}={}\amp 3 u+6\\ \noalign{\smallskip} y\amp =\amp u-v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} z\amp =\amp 5 u+v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} 2x-y-z\amp =\amp 12\end{array}\quad\Rightarrow\quad u=\frac{x-6}{3},\quad v=\frac{x-6}{3}-y \end{equation*}
We conclude that \(\mathrm{\Phi }( u,v )\) parametrizes the whole plane \(2x-y-z=12\text{.}\) (a) The tangent vectors \({\mathbf{T} }_u\) and \({\mathbf{T} }_v\) are:
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_u =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial u}=\frac{\partial }{\partial u}( 3 u+6,u-v,5 u+v )= \left\lt 3,1,5 \right> \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_v =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial v}=\frac{\partial }{\partial v}( 3 u+6,u-v,5 u+v )= \left\lt 0,-1,1 \right> \end{equation*}
The normal vector is the following cross product:
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{n} ( u,v )={\mathbf{T} }_u \times {\mathbf{T} }_v = \left|\begin{array}{rrr}\mathbf{i} \amp \mathbf{j} \amp \mathbf{k} \\ 3\amp 1\amp 5\\ 0\amp -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left|\begin{array}{rr}1\amp 5\\ -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{i} -\left|\begin{array}{rr}3\amp 5\\ 0\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{j} +\left|\begin{array}{rr}3\amp 1\\ 0\amp -1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{k}= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 6 \mathbf{i} -3 \mathbf{j} -3 \mathbf{k} = \left\lt 6,-3,-3 \right> \end{equation*}
(b) That area of \(S=\mathrm{\Phi }(\mathcal{D})\) is the following surface integral:
\begin{equation*} Area(S)=\iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \mathbf{n} ( u,v )\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \left\lt 6,-3,-3 \right>\| \,du \,dv= 3\sqrt{6}\iint_{\mathcal{D}} 1 \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 3\sqrt{6}\, \mathrm{Area} (\mathcal{D}) =3\sqrt{6}\cdot 4\cdot 2=24 \sqrt{6} \end{equation*}
(c) We express \(f( x,y,z )=yz\) in terms of the parameters \(u\) and \(v\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} f\left(\phi ( u,v )\right)=( u-v )(5 u+v )=5 u^2-4 uv- v^2 \end{equation*}
Using the Theorem on Surface Integrals we have:
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f( x,y,z ) \,dS = \iint_{\mathcal{D}}f\left(\phi ( u,v )\right)\| \mathbf{n} ( u,v )\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} \left(5 u^2-4uv- v^2\right)\| \left\lt 6,-3,-3 \right>\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 3\sqrt{6}\int_0^{2} \int_0^{4} \left(5 u^2-4uv- v^2\right) \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 3\sqrt{6}\int_0^{2} \left( \frac{5 u^3}3 -\frac{4u^2v}2- v^2u\right)\bigg|_{u=0}^{4} \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 3\sqrt{6}\int_0^{2} \left(\frac{320}3-\frac{64v}2-4 v^2\right) \,dv = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 3\sqrt{6}\left(\frac{320v}3-\frac{64v^2}4-\frac{4 v^3}3 \right)\bigg|_0^{2} =\frac{4992\sqrt{6}}{12} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \approx 1018.99 \end{equation*}
