Solution 12.12.4.4.1.

\begin{equation*} \mbox{div}\,{\vec{F}} = 3\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z. \end{equation*}
So, if \(W\) is the interior of the box, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{W}(3\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dV = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{1}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}(3\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating through each of the integrals in turn, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{1}^{3} 3\cdot 2x\,z + 3\cdot 2y\,z + 2z^{2}\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=2}\, dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{1}^{3} 12 x + 12 y + 8\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{2} 12 x y + 6 y^2 + 8 y\bigg|_{y=1}^{y=3}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{2} 24 x + 48 + 16\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 12 x^2 + 48 x + 16 x\bigg|_{x=0}^{x=2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 176. \end{equation*}
Finding flux directly, note that on the faces \(x=2\) and \(x=0\) the normal vectors to the surfaces are \(\vec i\) and \(-\vec i\) respectively, so that \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 3x^{2}\,dy\,dz\) (where \(x\) is evaluated at 2 or 0), and similarly on \(y = 3\) and \(y = 1\text{,}\) and \(z = 2\) and \(z = 0\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 3y^{2}\,dx\,dz\) and \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 2z^{2}\,dx\,dy\text{,}\) respectively. Thus on \(x = 0\) and \(z = 0\) the flux is zero. On the remaining faces, we have (1) On \(x = 2\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{1}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}\,12\,dz\,dy = 48\text{;}\) (2) On \(y = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{0}^{2}\,27\,dz\,dx = 108\text{;}\) (3) On \(y = 1\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{0}^{2}\,3\,dz\,dx = -12\text{;}\) and (4) On \(z = 2\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{2}\int_{1}^{3}\,8\,dy\,dx = 32\text{.}\) Thus the total flux is the sum of these, \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = 176\text{.}\)
