\(x=7 u+4\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=13 u+v\) satisfy the equation of the plane,
\begin{equation*} 2x-y-z =2(7 u+4 )-( u-v )-( 13 u+v )= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 14 u+8-u+v-13 u-v=8 \end{equation*}
Moreover, for any \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\) satisfying \(2x-y-z=8\text{,}\) there are values of \(u\) and \(v\) such that \(x=7 u+4\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=13 u+v\text{,}\) since the following equations can be solved for \(u\) and \(v\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl}x\amp {}={}\amp 7 u+4\\ \noalign{\smallskip} y\amp =\amp u-v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} z\amp =\amp 13 u+v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} 2x-y-z\amp =\amp 8\end{array}\quad\Rightarrow\quad u=\frac{x-4}{7},\quad v=\frac{x-4}{7}-y \end{equation*}
We conclude that \(\mathrm{\Phi }( u,v )\) parametrizes the whole plane \(2x-y-z=8\text{.}\) (a) The tangent vectors \({\mathbf{T} }_u\) and \({\mathbf{T} }_v\) are:
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_u =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial u}=\frac{\partial }{\partial u}( 7 u+4,u-v,13 u+v )= \left\lt 7,1,13 \right> \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_v =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial v}=\frac{\partial }{\partial v}( 7 u+4,u-v,13 u+v )= \left\lt 0,-1,1 \right> \end{equation*}
The normal vector is the following cross product:
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{n} ( u,v )={\mathbf{T} }_u \times {\mathbf{T} }_v = \left|\begin{array}{rrr}\mathbf{i} \amp \mathbf{j} \amp \mathbf{k} \\ 7\amp 1\amp 13\\ 0\amp -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left|\begin{array}{rr}1\amp 13\\ -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{i} -\left|\begin{array}{rr}7\amp 13\\ 0\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{j} +\left|\begin{array}{rr}7\amp 1\\ 0\amp -1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{k}= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 14 \mathbf{i} -7 \mathbf{j} -7 \mathbf{k} = \left\lt 14,-7,-7 \right> \end{equation*}
(b) That area of \(S=\mathrm{\Phi }(\mathcal{D})\) is the following surface integral:
\begin{equation*} Area(S)=\iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \mathbf{n} ( u,v )\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \left\lt 14,-7,-7 \right>\| \,du \,dv= 7\sqrt{6}\iint_{\mathcal{D}} 1 \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
