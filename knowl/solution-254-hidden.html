\begin{equation*} \mbox{div}\,{\vec{F}} = 4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2z. \end{equation*}
So, if \(W\) is the interior of the box, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{W}(4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2z)dV = \int_{2}^{3}\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{3}(4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2z)dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating through each of the integrals in turn, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{2}^{3}\int_{0}^{1} 4\cdot 2x\,z + 5\cdot 2y\,z + z^{2}\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=3}\, dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{2}^{3}\int_{0}^{1} 24 x + 30 y + 9\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{2}^{3} 24 x y + 15 y^2 + 9 y\bigg|_{y=0}^{y=1}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{2}^{3} 24 x + 15 + 9\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 12 x^2 + 15 x + 9 x\bigg|_{x=2}^{x=3} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 84. \end{equation*}
Finding flux directly, note that on the faces \(x=3\) and \(x=2\) the normal vectors to the surfaces are \(\vec i\) and \(-\vec i\) respectively, so that \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 4x^{2}\,dy\,dz\) (where \(x\) is evaluated at 3 or 2), and similarly on \(y = 1\) and \(y = 0\text{,}\) and \(z = 3\) and \(z = 0\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 5y^{2}\,dx\,dz\) and \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm z^{2}\,dx\,dy\text{,}\) respectively. Thus on \(y = 0\) and \(z = 0\) the flux is zero. On the remaining faces, we have (1) On \(x = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{3}\,36\,dz\,dy = 108\text{;}\) (2) On \(x = 2\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{3}\,16\,dz\,dy = -48\text{;}\) (3) On \(y = 1\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{2}^{3}\int_{0}^{3}\,5\,dz\,dx = 15\text{;}\) and (4) On \(z = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{2}^{3}\int_{0}^{1}\,9\,dy\,dx = 9\text{.}\) Thus the total flux is the sum of these, \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = 84\text{.}\)
