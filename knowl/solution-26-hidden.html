(a) Let us suppose that \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along the line \(y=x\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} f(x,y) = f(x,x) = \frac{x^5}{x^{8} + x^4} = \frac{x}{x^4+1}. \end{equation*}
Therefore
\begin{equation*} \lim_{^{(x,y)\to(0,0)}_{y=x}} f(x,y) = \lim_{x\to0} \frac{x}{x^4+1} = 0. \end{equation*}
(b) On the other hand, if \((x,y)\) approaches \((0,0)\) along the curve \(y=x^4\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(x,y)=f(x,x^4) = \frac{x^{8}}{x^{8} + x^{16}} = 1 \end{equation*}
and so
\begin{equation*} \lim_{^{(x,y)\to(0,0)}_{y=x^4}} f(x,y) = \lim_{x\to0}f(x,x^4) = 1. \end{equation*}
Thus no matter how close they are to the origin, there will be points \((x,y)\) such that \(f(x,y)\) is close to 0 and points \((x,y)\) such that \(f(x,y)\) is close to \(1\text{.}\) So the limit
\begin{equation*} \lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} f(x,y) \end{equation*}
does not exist.
