Solution 10.2.5.9.1.

(a) Moving right from \(P\) in the direction of increasing \(x\) decreases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_x(P)\) is negative. Moving up from \(P\) in the direction of increasing \(y\) decreases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_y(P)\) is negative. (b) Moving right from \(Q\) in the direction of increasing \(x\) decreases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_x(Q)\) is negative. Moving up from \(Q\) in the direction of increasing \(y\) increases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_y(Q)\) is positive. (c) Moving right from \(R\) in the direction of increasing \(x\) increases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_x(R)\) is positive. Moving up from \(R\) in the direction of increasing \(y\) increases \(f\text{,}\) so \(f_y(R)\) is positive.
