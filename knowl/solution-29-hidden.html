\(f(20,6) = 90\text{.}\) (a) Thus, to estimate \(f_t(20,6)\text{,}\) we can take
\begin{equation*} f_t(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20+2,6) - f(20-2,6)}{4} = \frac{86 - 93}{4} = -1.75. \end{equation*}
Alternately, we could take either of the two one-sided difference quotients,
\begin{equation*} f_t(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20+2,6) - f(20,6)}{2} = -2 \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} f_t(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20,6) - f(20-2,6)}{2} = -1.5. \end{equation*}
In each case, the units of \(f_t\) are percent per month. This says 20 months after rats are exposed to a formaldehyde concentration of 6 ppm, the percent of rats surviving is decreasing at a rate of about -1.75 per month. In other words, during the 20 month, around an additional -1.75 percent of the rats will die. (b) Proceeding similarly to find \(f_c(20,6)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} f_c(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20,6+2) - f(20,6-2)}{4} = \frac{70 - 97}{4} = -2.07692, \end{equation*}
or, with one-sided difference quotients,
\begin{equation*} f_c(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20,6+2) - f(20,6)}{2} = -2.22222 \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} f_c(20,6) \approx \frac{f(20,6) - f(20,6-2)}{2} = -1.75. \end{equation*}
These indicate that if the original concentration increases from 6 by 1 ppm, the percent surviving after 20 months decreases by about 2.08.
