Solution 10.5.4.5.1.

\(z\) is a function of two variables \(x\) and \(y\) which are functions of two variables \(u\) and \(v\text{,}\) the two chain rule identities which apply are:
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\frac{\partial x}{\partial u}+ \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\frac{\partial y}{\partial u} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\frac{\partial x}{\partial v}+ \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\frac{\partial y}{\partial v} \end{equation*}
This problem is most easily solved by substitution:
\begin{equation*} z = \sin\!\left(\left(v\cos\!\left(u\right)\right)^{2}+\left(v\sin\!\left(u\right)\right)^{2}\right) = \sin\!\left(v^{2}\right) \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} = \cos\!\left(v^{2}\right)\cdot 2v. \end{equation*}
This problem can also be solved using the chain rule but it is more difficult.
