Let \(T\) be the temperature function and \(P\) the point. The value of the greatest rate of increase is the magnitude of the gradient. Thus \(\left| \nabla T (P) \right| = 5\text{.}\)

The gradient points in the northeast direction, so \(\nabla T (P) = \left \langle 5 \cos (45^o) , 5 \sin (45^o) \right \rangle = \left \langle \frac{5}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{5}{\sqrt{2}} \right \rangle\text{.}\)

A unit vector in the north direction is \(\mathbf{u} = \langle 0, 1 \rangle\text{,}\) so the rate of change of increase of the temperature in that direction is

\begin{equation*} D_{\mathbf u} T(P) = \nabla T (P) \cdot \mathbf{u} = \frac{5}{\sqrt{2}} \end{equation*}
