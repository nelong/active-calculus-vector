Solution 10.7.5.13.1.

\(x,y, z\) cm. Then the volume is given by \(V = xyz = 30\text{.}\) The surface area \(S\) is given by
\begin{equation*} S = 2xy+2xz+2yz. \end{equation*}
Substituting \(z = 30/(xy)\) gives
\begin{equation*} S = 2xy +\frac{60}{y} + \frac{60}{x}. \end{equation*}
At a critical point,
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} = 2y - \frac{60}{x^2} = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} = 2x - \frac{60}{y^2} = 0. \end{equation*}
The symmetry of the equations (or by dividing the equations) tells us that \(x=y\) and so that
\begin{equation*} 2x - \frac{60}{x^2} = 0, \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} x^3 = 30, \quad\mbox{and so}\quad x = 30^{1/3}\mbox{ cm}. \end{equation*}
Thus the only critical point is \(x = y = (30)^{1/3}\) cm, and \(z = 30/\left ( (30)^{1/3} \cdot (30)^{1/3} \right ) = (30)^{1/3}\) cm. At the critical point
\begin{equation*} S_{xx} S_{yy}-(S_{xy})^2= \frac{120}{x^3}\cdot\frac{120}{y^3}-2^2= \frac{(120)^2}{x^{3}y^{3}}-4. \end{equation*}
Since \(D>0\) and \(S_{xx}>0\) at this critical point, the critical point \(x = y = z = (30)^{1/3}\) is a local minimum. Since \(S\rightarrow\infty\) as \(x,y\rightarrow\infty\text{,}\) the local minimum is a global minimum.
