Solution 10.7.5.13.1.

\(x\) and \(y\) cm. Let the height be \(z\) cm. Then the volume is given by \(xyz = 34\) and the surface area, \(S\text{,}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} S = xy + 2xz + 2yz. \end{equation*}
Substituting \(z = 34/(xy)\) gives
\begin{equation*} S = xy + \frac{68}{y} + \frac{68}{x}. \end{equation*}
At a critical point
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} = y - \frac{68}{x^2} = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} = x - \frac{68}{y^2} = 0. \end{equation*}
The symmetry of the equations tells us that \(x=y\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} x - \frac{68}{x^2} = 0, \quad\mbox{and}\quad x^3 = 68, \end{equation*}
so that \(x = (68)^{1/3}\) cm. Thus the only critical point is \(x=y=(68)^{1/3}\) cm and \(z = 34/((68)^{1/3}\cdot (68)^{1/3}) = (17/2)^{1/3}\) cm. At the critical point
\begin{equation*} D = S_{xx}S_{yy}-(S_{xy})^2 =\frac{136}{x^3} \cdot \frac{136}{y^3}-1^2= \frac{136^2}{x^3y^3}-1. \end{equation*}
Since \(D>0\) and \(S_{xx}>0\) at this critical point, the critical point \(x = y = (68)^{1/3}\text{,}\) \(z = (17/2)^{1/3}\) is a local minimum. Since \(S\rightarrow\infty\) as \(x,y\rightarrow\infty\text{,}\) the local minimum is a global minimum.
