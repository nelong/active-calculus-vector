\((x,y,z)\) to the origin:
\begin{equation*} S=x^2+y^2+z^2. \end{equation*}
Since \(z^2 = 73 - x y - 9 x\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} S = x^2 + y^2 + 73 - x y - 9 x. \end{equation*}
At a critical point
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} = 2 x - y - 9 = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} = 2 y - x = 0, \end{equation*}
so \(x = 2 y\text{,}\) and
\begin{equation*} 2(2 y) - y - 9 = 0 \end{equation*}
giving \(y=3\text{,}\) so \(x=6\) and \(z^2 = 73 - (6)(3) - 9 (6)\text{,}\) so \(z=\pm 1\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation*} D= S_{xx}S_{yy}-(S_{xy})^2 =2\cdot2 - (-1)^2 = 4 - 1 > 0, \end{equation*}
so, since \(D>0\) and \(S_{xx}>0\text{,}\) the critical points are local minima. Since \(S\rightarrow\infty\) as \(x,y\rightarrow\pm\infty\text{,}\) the local minima are global minima. If \(x=6\text{,}\) \(y=3\text{,}\) \(z=\pm 1\text{,}\) we have \(S = 6^2 + 3^2 + 1^2 = 46\text{,}\) so the shortest distance to the origin is \(\sqrt{46}\text{.}\)
