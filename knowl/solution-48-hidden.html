\begin{equation*} C = 4(hl+wh) + 6 wl. \end{equation*}
But the volume \(= wlh = 522\text{,}\) so \(l = 522/(wh)\text{,}\) thus
\begin{equation*} C = \frac{2088}{w} + 4 wh + \frac{3132}{h}. \end{equation*}
To minimize \(C\text{,}\) we find the critical points of \(C\) by solving
\begin{equation*} C_h = 4 w - \frac{3132}{h^2} = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} C_w = 4 h - \frac{2088}{w^2} = 0. \end{equation*}
We get
\begin{equation*} 4 w h^2 = 3132, \quad\mbox{and}\quad 4 h w^2 = 2088, \end{equation*}
Since \(w\text{,}\) \(h \ne 0\text{,}\) we can divide the first equation by the second, giving
\begin{equation*} \frac{4 w h^2}{4 h w^2} = \frac{3132}{2088}, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \frac{h}{w} = \frac{3}{2}, \end{equation*}
thus
\begin{equation*} h = \frac{3}{2} w. \end{equation*}
Substituting this into \(C_w = 0\text{,}\) we obtain \(w^3 = 174\text{,}\) so \(w = 7.03387\) and \(h = 10.5508\) cm. Then \(l = 522/(wh) = 7.03381\) cm. Next we can check that these dimensions minimize the cost C. We find that
\begin{equation*} D=C_{hh}C_{ww}-C^2_{hw} = (\frac{6264}{h^3})(\frac{4176}{w^3}) - 4^2, \end{equation*}
So that at the critical point, \(D > 0\) and \(C_{hh} > 0\text{,}\) and thus \(C\) has a local minimum at \(h=10.5508\) and \(w=7.03387\text{.}\) Since \(C\) increases without bound as \(w,h\to 0\) or \(\infty\text{,}\) this local minimum must be a global minimum. Therefore, the dimensions of the box that minimize the cost are \(w=7.03387\) cm, \(l=7.03381\) cm and \(h=10.5508\) cm.
