Solution 10.8.3.3.1.

\(f\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} f_x = 2xy = 0, \quad f_y = x^2 + 12 y - 1 = 0. \end{equation*}
From the first equation, we get either \(x=0\) or \(y=0\text{.}\) If \(x=0\text{,}\) from the second equation we get \(12 y - 1 = 0\text{,}\) so \(y=1/12\text{.}\) If instead \(y=0\text{,}\) then from the second equation \(x=\pm 1\text{.}\) We conclude that the critical points are \((0,1/12)\text{,}\) \((1,0)\text{,}\) and \((-1,0)\text{.}\) All three critical points satisfy the constraint \(x^2+y^2\le 54.3333333333333\text{.}\) The Lagrange conditions, \(\nabla f= \lambda\nabla g\text{,}\) are:
\begin{equation*} 2xy=\lambda 2x,\qquad x^2 + 12 y - 1 =\lambda 2y. \end{equation*}
From the first equation, when \(x\neq 0\text{,}\) we divide by \(2x\) to get \(\lambda=y\text{.}\) Substituting into the second equation, we get
\begin{equation*} x^2 + 12 y - 1 = 2y^2. \end{equation*}
Then using \(x^2 = 54.3333333333333 - y^2\) from the constraint, we have
\begin{equation*} 54.3333333333333 - y^2 + 12 y - 1 = 2 y^2, \end{equation*}
so \(3 y^2 - 12 y - 53.3333333333333 = 0\text{.}\) Factoring, we get \((3y + 8 )( y - 6.66666666666667 ) = 0\text{,}\) so \(y = -2.66666666666667\) or \(y = 6.66666666666667\text{.}\) From the constraint, we get \(x=\pm \sqrt{47.2222222222222}\) when \(y=-2.66666666666667\) and \(x=\pm \sqrt{9.88888888888887}\) for \(y=6.66666666666667\text{.}\) If instead \(x=0\text{,}\) so that we cannot divide by \(x\) in the first Lagrange equation, then from the constraint, \(y=\pm \sqrt{54.3333333333333}\text{.}\) Summarizing, the following points are either critical points or satisfy the Lagrange conditions:
\begin{equation*} (\pm 1,0),(0,1/12),(\pm \sqrt{47.2222222222222}, -2.66666666666667),(\pm \sqrt{9.88888888888887}, 6.66666666666667), (0, -\sqrt{54.3333333333333}), \mbox{ and } (0, \sqrt{54.3333333333333}). \end{equation*}
These are the candidates for global maximum or minimum points. The corresponding values for \(f(x,y)=x^2 y + 6 y^2 - y\) are:
\begin{equation*} 0, -0.042, -80.593, 325.926, 333.371, \mbox{ and } 318.629. \end{equation*}
The largest value is \(333.371\) at the point \((0,-\sqrt{54.3333333333333})\text{,}\) and the smallest value is \(-80.5926\) at \((\pm \sqrt{47.2222222222222},-2.66666666666667)\text{,}\)
