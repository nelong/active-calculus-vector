(a) The critical points of \(h(x,y)\) occur where
\begin{equation*} h_x(x,y) = 2 x - 4 \lambda = 0 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} h_y(x,y) = 2 y - 6 \lambda = 0. \end{equation*}
The only critical point is \((x,y) = (2 \lambda, 3 \lambda)\) and it gives a minimum value for \(h(x,y)\text{.}\) That minimum value is
\begin{equation*} m(\lambda) = h(2 \lambda, 3 \lambda) = 4 \lambda^2 + 9 \lambda^2 - \lambda( 8 \lambda + 18 \lambda - 19 ) \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} m(\lambda) = -13 \lambda^2 + 19 \lambda. \end{equation*}
(b) The maximum value of \(m(\lambda)= -13 \lambda^2 + 19\lambda\) occurs at a critical point, where \(m'(\lambda) = -26 \lambda + 19 = 0\text{.}\) At this point, \(\lambda = 0.730769\) and \(m(0.730769)= 6.94231\text{.}\) (c) We want to minimize \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\) subject to the constraint \(g(x,y)=19\text{,}\) where \(g(x,y) = 4 x + 6 y\text{.}\) The method of Lagrange multipliers has us solve
\begin{equation*} 2x = 4\lambda, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2y = 6\lambda, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} 4 x + 6 y = 19. \end{equation*}
We can see that this gives the same solutions for \(x\) and \(y\) as we obtained in part (a), and then, plugging in to the third equation, we have
\begin{equation*} 8 \lambda + 18 \lambda = 19. \end{equation*}
Thus we get the same \(\lambda\) as before: \(\lambda = 0.730769\text{,}\) so that the minimum value of \(f\) is \(f(2\lambda, 3\lambda)\text{,}\) where \(\lambda = 0.730769\text{,}\) or \(f_{\mbox{min}} = 6.94231\text{.}\) Note that the two question have the same answer. This makes sense, because in the first problem the largest minimum value will clearly occur when \(4 x + 6 y = 19\text{,}\) which is the constraint in the third equation.
