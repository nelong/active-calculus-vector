Solution 11.3.3.5.1.

(a) The contour \(f(x,y)=1\) lies in the \(xy\)-plane and has equation
\begin{equation*} 7 e^{-(x-3)^2-y^2} = 1, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} -(x-3)^2-y^2 = \ln (1/7),\quad\mbox{or}\quad (x-3)^2+y^2 = \ln 7. \end{equation*}
This is the equation of a circle of radius \(\sqrt{\ln(7)}\) centered at \((3,0)\) in the \(xy\)-plane. Similarly, for \(k \lt 7\text{,}\) we have that the contours \(f(x,y) = k\) are the circles \((x - 3)^2 + y^2 = \ln(7/k)\text{,}\) all of which are centered on \((3,0)\text{.}\) (b) The cross-section has equation \(z=f(3,y)=7 e^{-y^2}\text{.}\) If \(x=3\text{,}\) the base region in the \(xy\)-plane extends from \(y=-\sqrt{7}\) to \(y=\sqrt{7}\text{.}\) Thus the area is
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Area } = \int_{-\sqrt{7}}^{\sqrt{7}}7 e^{-y^2}\,dy. \end{equation*}
(c) Using this idea and slicing parallel to the \(y\)-axis, we get
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume } = \int_{-4}^{4}\int_{-\sqrt{16-x^2}}^{\sqrt{16-x^2}} 7 e^{-(x-3)^2-y^2}\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
