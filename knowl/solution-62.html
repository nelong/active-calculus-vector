Solution 11.4.6.10.1.

(a) \(p\) is not a joint density function. Since \(p(x,y)=0\) outside the region \(R\text{,}\) the volume under the graph of \(p\) is the same as the volume under the graph of \(p\) over the region \(R\text{,}\) which is \(0.5\text{,}\) not one, which it would have to be if \(p(x,y)\) were a joint density function.. (b) \(p\) is not a joint density function. Since \(p(x,y)=0\) outside the region \(R\text{,}\) the volume under the graph of \(p\) is the same as the volume under the graph of \(p\) over the region \(R\text{,}\) which is \(4\text{,}\) . Finally, for a constant function to be a joint density function on \(R\) given by \(2 \le x \le 3, 0 \le y \le 2\text{,}\) the volume under the constant must be one. So, if \(p(x,y) = k\text{,}\) \(2 k = 1\text{,}\) so \(p(x,y) = \frac1{2}\text{.}\)
