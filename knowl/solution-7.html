Solution 9.2.8.4.1.

(a) The components are \(v_1 = 5 \cos 3\pi/4 = (5)(-{1\over\sqrt 2}) = -{5 \over\sqrt 2}\) and \(v_2 = 5 \sin 3\pi/4 = (5)({1\over\sqrt 2}) = {5 \over\sqrt 2}\text{.}\) Thus \(\vec v = -{5 \over\sqrt 2}\,\vec i + {5 \over\sqrt 2}\,\vec j\text{.}\) (b) Since the vector lies in the \(yz\)-plane, its \(x\)-component is 0. Its \(y\)-component is \((3)(\cos \pi/6) = {3\sqrt 3 \over 2}\text{,}\) and and its \(z\)-component is \((3)(\sin \pi/6) = {3 \over 2}\text{.}\) So the vector is \(\vec w = {3\sqrt 3 \over 2}\,\vec j + {3 \over 2}\,\vec k\text{.}\)
