Solution 11.6.4.4.1.

\(z=10\sqrt{x-2}\text{,}\) \(2\le x\le 4\text{,}\) around the \(z\)-axis is shown in the figure below.
At height \(z\text{,}\) the cross-section is a horizontal circle of radius \(a\text{.}\) Thus, letting the angle \(\theta\) around the \(z\)-axis be \(t\text{,}\) a point on this horizontal circle is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec r=a \cos t \vec i + a \sin t \vec j + z\vec k. \end{equation*}
However, the radius \(a\) and height \(z\) are not independent. We can parameterize easily in two ways: first, suppose that we take the radius \(a\) to be our second parameter, \(a = s\text{.}\) Then, the height \(z = 10\sqrt{s - 2}\text{,}\) and our parameterization is
\begin{equation*} x(s,t) = s\cos t, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y(s,t) = s\sin t, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} z(s,t) = 10\sqrt{s - 2}, \end{equation*}
with
\begin{equation*} 2\le s\le 4 \quad\mbox{and}\quad 0\le t\le 2\pi. \end{equation*}
Alternately, we could take the height \(z\) to be the second parameter, \(z = s\text{.}\) Then, solving for the radius \(r\) in the expression for the curve, we get \(a = \left(\frac{s}{10}\right)^2 + 2\text{.}\) Thus in this case the parameterization is
\begin{equation*} x(s,t) = \left((\frac{s}{10})^2 + 2\right)\,\cos(t), \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y(s,t) = \left((\frac{s}{10})^2 + 2\right)\,\sin(t), \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} z(s,t) = s, \end{equation*}
with
\begin{equation*} 0\le s\le 10\sqrt{2}\quad\mbox{and}\quad 0\le t\le 2\pi. \end{equation*}
