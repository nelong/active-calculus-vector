(a) Let \(z\) be measured from the bottom of the post, and the center of the post be at \(x = 0\text{,}\) \(y = 0\text{.}\) The surface is the revolution surface obtained by revolving a curve extending vertically along one edge of the surface around the \(z\) axis, as suggested by the figure below.
From the measurements given, we obtain the equation of the curve
\begin{equation*} x = \cos \left( \frac{\pi}{3} z \right) + 2, \qquad 0 \leq z \leq 24. \end{equation*}
Rotating this around the \(z\)-axis, taking \(z=s\) and \(\theta = t\) as the parameters, we get the parametric equations
\begin{equation*} x = (\cos \left( \frac{\pi}{3} s \right)+2)\cos t, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y = (\cos \left( \frac{\pi}{3} s \right)+2)\sin t, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} z = s, \end{equation*}
with
\begin{equation*} 0 \leq s \leq 24, \qquad 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi. \end{equation*}
(b) We know that the points in the curve consists of cross-sections of circles parallel to the \(xy\) plane and of radius \(\cos((\pi/{3})z+2)\text{.}\) Thus,
\begin{equation*} \mbox{Area of cross-section} = \pi\left(\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{3}z+2\right)\right)^2 \end{equation*}
Integrating over \(z\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume } = \pi \int_0^{24} \left(\cos\frac{\pi}{3} z+2\right)^2 dz = \pi \int_0^{24} \left(\cos^2\frac{\pi}{3}z+4\cos\frac{\pi}{3}z +4 \right) dz = 108 \pi \mbox{ in}^3. \end{equation*}
