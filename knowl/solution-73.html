Solution 11.7.3.6.1.

\(z = 8 - 2 x - 4 y\text{.}\) Note that the projection of this into the \(xy\)-plane is the triangular region below \(y = 2 - \frac{1}{2} x\text{.}\) The mass of the volume is then
\begin{equation*} M = \int_R \delta\,dV = \int_0^4\int_0^{2 - \frac{1}{2} x}\int_0^{8-2 x-4 y}\,x + 4 y\,dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating with respect to \(z\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} M = \int_0^4\int_0^{2 - \frac{1}{2} x} (x + 4 y)(8-2 x-4 y)\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} M = \int_0^4\int_0^{2 - \frac{1}{2} x} -2 x^2 + 8 x - 4 x y - 8 x y + 32 y - 16 y^2\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Then, integrating with respect to \(y\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} M = \int_0^4 (-2 x^2 + 8 x)(2 - \frac{1}{2} x) + \frac12(32 - 12 x)(2 - \frac{1}{2} x)^2 - \frac{16}{3} (2 - \frac{1}{2} x)^3\,dx. \end{equation*}
Expanding the these terms, we have
\begin{equation*} M = \int_0^4 \frac{1}{6} x^3 + 0 x^2 - 8 x + \frac{64}{3}\, dx = 32. \end{equation*}
