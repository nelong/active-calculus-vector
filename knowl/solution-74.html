Solution 11.7.3.7.1.

\(V\) of the solid is \(4 \cdot 5 \cdot 3 = 60\text{.}\) Thus for \(I_x\) we have
\begin{equation*} I_x = \frac{120}{60}\int_W\, y^2 + z^2\,dV = 2\int_0^4\int_0^5\int_0^3\,y^2 + z^2\,dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
This is
\begin{equation*} I_x = 2\int_0^4\int_0^5 y^2 z + \frac13 z^3\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=3} = 2\int_0^{4}\int_0^{5}\, 3 y^2 + 9\, dy\,dx, \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} I_x = 2\int_0^4 125 + 45\, dx = 1360. \end{equation*}
Similarly,
\begin{equation*} I_y = 1000,\qquad\mbox{and}\qquad I_z = 1640. \end{equation*}
