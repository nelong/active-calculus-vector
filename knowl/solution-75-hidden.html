\(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3}\pi\text{,}\) so the total mass is \(\frac{5}{3} \pi\) (since the density is always 5). The center of mass \(z\)-coordinate is given by
\begin{equation*} \overline{z} = \frac{3}{5 \pi} \int_C z \,dV \end{equation*}
Using cylindrical coordinates to evaluate this integral gives
\begin{equation*} \overline{z} = \frac{3}{5 \pi} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{1} \int_0^z 5 z r\,dr \,dz \,d\theta, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \overline{z} = \frac{3}{5 \pi} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{1} 5\cdot \frac{z^3}{2} \,dz \,d\theta = \left(\frac{3}{5 \pi}\right)\left(2\pi\right) 5\cdot \frac{1}{8} = \frac{3}{4}\mbox{ cm}. \end{equation*}
