Solution 11.9.5.6.1.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc}s\amp =\amp xy\\t\amp =\amp xy^2,\end{array}. \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial(s,t)}{\partial(x,y)}= \left| \begin{array}{ccc} \frac{\partial s}{\partial x} \amp \frac{\partial s}{\partial y}\\ \frac{\partial t}{\partial x} \amp \frac{\partial t}{\partial y} \end{array}\right| =\left|\begin{array}{ccc}y\amp x\\y^2\amp 2xy\end{array}\right|=xy^2=t. \end{equation*}
Since
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial(s,t)}{\partial(x,y)}\cdot \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}=1, \qquad \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}=\frac{1}{t}. \end{equation*}
So
\begin{equation*} \int_R xy^2\,dA=\int_{T}t\left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right|\,ds\,dt= \int_{T}t\,(\frac 1t)\,ds\,dt=\int_{T}\,ds\,dt, \end{equation*}
where \(T\) is the region bounded by \(s=2\text{,}\) \(s=5\text{,}\) \(t=2\text{,}\) \(t=5\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_Rxy^2\,dA=\int_2^{5}\,ds\int_2^{5}\,dt=9. \end{equation*}
