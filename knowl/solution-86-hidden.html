\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc} x \amp = \amp 5 s + t\\ y \amp = \amp s - 2 t, \end{array} \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right| = \left|\begin{array}{cc} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} \amp \frac{\partial x}{\partial t}\\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial s} \amp \frac{\partial y}{\partial t} \end{array}\right| =\left|\begin{array}{cc} 5 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp -2 \end{array}\right| = |-11|, \end{equation*}
hence
\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right| = 11. \end{equation*}
We therefore get
\begin{equation*} \int_R (x+y) \,dA = \int_{T} ((5 s + t) + (s - 2 t)) \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right|dsdt = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int_{T}(6s-t)(11)\,ds\,dt=\int_{T} (11\!\left(6s-t\right))\,ds\,dt, \end{equation*}
where \(T\) is the region in the \(st\)-plane corresponding to \(R\text{.}\) Now, we need to find \(T\text{.}\) Because
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc} x \amp = \amp 5 s+ t \\ y \amp = \amp s - 2 t \end{array} \end{equation*}
we can use the boundaries of the region \(R\) to find the boundaries of \(T\text{.}\) Using the four vertices of the parallelogram \(R\text{,}\) we the edges \(y = \frac1{5}\,x\text{,}\) \(y = -2\,x\text{,}\) \(y = \frac{1}{5}\,(x-1) - 2\) and \(y = -2(x - 5) + 1\text{.}\) So, from the above transformation, the boundaries are \(t = 0\text{,}\) \(s = 0\text{,}\) \(t = 1\) and \(s = 1\text{.}\) Therefore
\begin{equation*} \int_R(x+y)\,dA = \int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{1}\, 11\!\left(6s-t\right) \,ds\,dt = \frac{55}{2}. \end{equation*}
(Alternately, we could of course also write the integral as \(\int_{1}^{0} \int_{1}^{0}\, 11\!\left(6s-t\right) \,ds\,dt\text{.}\))
