Task 12.12.8.b.
In this task, we will calculate the triple integral on the right side of the Divergence Theorem for our example. Since \(\vF=\langle xy-z, yz+e^x,z(x-y) \rangle \text{,}\) we can calculate \(\divg(\vF)= \dfrac{\partial}{\partial x}(xy-z)+\dfrac{\partial}{\partial y}(yz-e^x)+\dfrac{\partial}{\partial z}(z(x-y)) = y+z+(x-y)\text{.}\) The region \(Q\) can be described by the bounds \(0\leq x\leq 1\text{,}\) \(0\leq y\leq 1-x\text{,}\) and \(0\leq z\leq 1-x-y\text{.}\) Thus, our triple integral will be setup and evaluated by
\begin{gather*} \int_0^1 \int_0^{1-x} \int_0^{1-x-y} y+z+x \enspace dz \enspace dy \enspace dx \\ \int_0^1 \int_0^{1-x} y(1-x-y)+1/2(1-x-y)+x(1-x-y) \enspace dy \enspace dx \\ \int_0^1 \frac{1}{12} (4x^3-3x^2-6x+5) \enspace dx \end{gather*}
which will evaluate to \(\frac{1}{12}\text{.}\) This matches with the flux calculations we did in the previous task to verify the Divergence Theorem for our example.
