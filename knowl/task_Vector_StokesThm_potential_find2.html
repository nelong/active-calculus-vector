When finding an anti-derivative of a function of a single variable, you know that there is an infinite family of anti-derivatives, but that any two anti-derivatives differ by a constant. This is why we write expressions such as \(\displaystyle\int\cos(x)\, dx = \sin(x) + C\text{.}\) A similar phenomenon occurs with (scalar) potential functions for gradient vector fields.
