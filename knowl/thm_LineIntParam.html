Theorem 12.3.5.

Let \(C\) be a smooth, oriented curve traced out by the vector-valued function \(\vr(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{.}\) If \(\vF\) is a continuous vector field defined near \(C\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int\limits_C \vF\cdot d\vr = \int\limits_a^b \vF(\vr(t))\cdot \frac{d\vr}{dt}(t)\, dt \end{equation*}
