Activity 11.4.4.

As we saw in Activity 11.3.5, the wind chill \(w(v,T)\text{,}\) in degrees Fahrenheit, is a function of the wind speed, in miles per hour, and the air temperature, in degrees Fahrenheit. Some values of the wind chill are recorded in Table 11.4.7.
  1. Estimate the partial derivatives \(w_{T}(20,-15)\text{,}\) \(w_{T}(20,-10)\text{,}\) and \(w_T(20,-5)\text{.}\) Use these results to estimate the second-order partial \(w_{TT}(20, -10)\text{.}\)
  2. In a similar way, estimate the second-order partial \(w_{vv}(20,-10)\text{.}\)
  3. Estimate the partial derivatives \(w_T(20,-10)\text{,}\) \(w_T(25,-10)\text{,}\) and \(w_T(15,-10)\text{,}\) and use your results to estimate the partial \(w_{Tv}(20,-10)\text{.}\)
  4. In a similar way, estimate the partial derivative \(w_{vT}(20,-10)\text{.}\)
  5. Write several sentences that explain what the values \(w_{TT}(20, -10)\text{,}\) \(w_{vv}(20,-10)\text{,}\) and \(w_{Tv}(20,-10)\) indicate regarding the behavior of \(w(v,T)\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. We approximate \(w_T(20,-15)\) using a symmetric difference quotient:
    \begin{equation*} w_T(20,-15) \approx \frac{w(20,-10)-w(20,-20)}{10} = \frac{-35-(-48)}{10} = 1.3\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Similarly,
    \begin{equation*} w_T(20,-10) \approx \frac{w(20,-5)-w(20,-15)}{10} = \frac{-29-(-42)}{10} = 1.3 \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} w_T(20,-5) \approx \frac{w(20,0)-w(20,-10)}{10} = \frac{-22-(-35)}{10} = 1.3\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Now \(w_{TT}(20,-10)\) can also be approximated with a symmetric difference quotient as
    \begin{equation*} w_{TT}(20,-10) \approx \frac{w_T(20,-5) - w_T(20,-15)}{10} \approx \frac{1.3-1.3}{10} = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. To approximate \(w_{vv}(20,-10)\) we will use a symmetric difference quotient with \(w_v(25,-10)\) and \(w_v(15,-10)\text{.}\) Now
    \begin{gather*} w_v(25,-10) \approx \frac{w(30,-10) - w(20,-10)}{10} = \frac{-39-(-35)}{10} = -0.4\\ w_v(15,-10) \approx \frac{w(20,-10) - w(10,-10)}{10} = \frac{-35-(-32)}{10} = -0.3\text{,} \end{gather*}
    so
    \begin{equation*} w_{vv}(20,-10) \approx \frac{w_v(25,-10) - w_v(15,-10)}{10} = \frac{-0.4-(-0.3)}{10} = -0.01\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. We already have \(w_T(20,-10) \approx 1.3\text{,}\) and similar calculations show that
    \begin{gather*} w_T(15,-10) \approx \frac{w(15,-5)-w(15,-15)}{10} = \frac{-26-(-39)}{10} = 1.3\\ w_T(25,-10) \approx \frac{w(25,-5)-w(25,-15)}{10} = \frac{-31-(-44)}{10} = 1.3\text{.} \end{gather*}
    So
    \begin{equation*} w_{Tv}(20,-10) \approx \frac{w_T(25,-10) - w_T(15,-10)}{10} \approx 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. To estimate \(w_{vT}(20,-10)\) we use a symmetric difference quotient with \(w_v(20,-15)\) and \(w_v(20,-5)\text{.}\) Now
    \begin{gather*} w_v(20,-15) \approx \frac{w(25,-15)-w(15,-15)}{10} = \frac{-44-(-39)}{10} = -0.5\\ w_v(20,-5) \approx \frac{w(25,-5)-w(15,-5)}{10} = \frac{-31-(-26)}{10} = -0.5\text{.} \end{gather*}
    So
    \begin{equation*} w_{vT}(20,-10) \approx \frac{w_v(20,-5) - w_v(20,-15)}{10} \approx 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
  5. The fact that \(w_{TT}(20, -10) \approx 0.03\) means that for every one \(^{\circ}F\) increase from \(-10\) degrees, the rate at which the wind chill changes as the temperature increases grows by approximately 0.03 \(\frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}\) if the wind speed is 20 miles per hour. The fact that \(w_{vv}(20, -10) \approx -0.01\) means that for every one mile per hour increase in the wind speed from 20 miles per hour, at a temperature of \(-10^{\circ}F\text{,}\) the rate at which the wind chill changes as the wind speed increases declines by approximately 0.01 \(\frac{^{\circ}F}{\text{ mile/hour } }\text{.}\) The fact that \(w_{Tv}(20, -10) \approx 0\) means that there is no change in the rate at which the wind chill changes as the temperature increases if the wind speed increases from 20 miles per hour and the temperature is \(-10^{\circ}F\text{.}\)
