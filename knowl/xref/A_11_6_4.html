Activity 12.6.3.

Consider the cylinder with radius \(a\) and height \(h\) defined parametrically by
\begin{equation*} \vr(s,t) = a\cos(s) \vi + a\sin(s) \vj + t \vk \end{equation*}
for \(0 \leq s \leq 2\pi\) and \(0 \leq t \leq h\text{,}\) as shown in FigureÂ 12.6.7.
  1. Set up an iterated integral to determine the surface area of this cylinder.
  2. Evaluate the iterated integral.
  3. Recall that one way to think about the surface area of a cylinder is to cut the cylinder horizontally and find the perimeter of the resulting cross sectional circle, then multiply by the height. Calculate the surface area of the given cylinder using this alternate approach, and compare your work in (b).
Solution.
  1. We have
    \begin{equation*} \vr_s(s,t) = -a\sin(s) \vi + a\cos(s) \vj \ \text{ and } \ \vr_t(s,t) = \vk\text{,} \end{equation*}
    so the area of the surface of the cylinder is
    \begin{equation*} \int \int_D |\vr_s \times \vr_t| \, dA = \int \int_D a \, dA\text{.} \end{equation*}
    In this case, \(dA = ds \ dt\text{,}\) so an iterated integral that represents the area of the surface is
    \begin{equation*} \int \int_D |\vr_s \times \vr_t| \, dA = \int_{0}^{h} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} a \, ds \, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. We can find the surface area of the cylinder by multiplying the circumference of the circle of radius \(a\) by the height \(h\) to obtain \(2 \pi a h\text{.}\) Evaluating the iterated integral yields
    \begin{align*} \int_{0}^{h} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} a^2 \, ds \, dt \amp = a \int_{0}^{h} \left. s \right|_{0}^{2 \pi} \, dt\\ \amp = 2 \pi a \int_{0}^{h} \, dt\\ \amp = 2 \pi a \left. t \right|_{0}^{h}\\ \amp = 2 \pi a h\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. If we slice the cylinder horizontally, a cross section is a circle of radius \(a\text{.}\) The circumference of this circle is \(2 \pi a\text{.}\) We multiply by the height of the cylinder to obtain the surface area as \(2 \pi a h\) as expected.
