Activity 10.2.3.

For each of the following vector-valued functions, find \(\vr'(t)\text{.}\)
  1. \(\vr(t) = \langle \cos(t), t\sin(t), \ln(t) \rangle\text{.}\)
  2. \(\vr(t) = \langle t^2 + 3t, e^{-2t}, \frac{t}{t^2 + 1} \rangle\text{.}\)
  3. \(\vr(t) = \langle \tan(t), \cos(t^2), te^{-t} \rangle\text{.}\)
  4. \(\vr(t) = \langle \sqrt{t^4 + 4}, \sin(3t), \cos(4t) \rangle\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. Differentiating componentwise gives
    \begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = \left\langle -\sin(t), t\cos(t)+\sin(t), \frac{1}{t} \right\rangle\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Differentiating componentwise gives
    \begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = \left\langle 2t+3, -2e^{-2t}, \frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2} \right\rangle\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. Differentiating componentwise gives
    \begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = \left\langle \sec^2(t), -2t\sin(t^2), -te^{-t}+e^{-t} \right\rangle\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. Differentiating componentwise gives
    \begin{equation*} \vr'(t) = \left\langle \frac{2t^3}{\sqrt{t^4 + 4}}, 3\cos(3t), -4\sin(4t) \right\rangle\text{.} \end{equation*}
