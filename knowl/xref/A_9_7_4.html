Activity 10.2.5.

Let
\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = \cos(t) \vi - \sin(t) \vj + t \vk. \end{equation*}
Sketch the curve using some appropriate tool.
  1. Determine the coordinates of the point on the curve traced out by \(\vr(t)\) when \(t = \pi\text{.}\)
  2. Find a direction vector for the line tangent to the graph of \(\vr\) at the point where \(t=\pi\text{.}\)
  3. Find the parametric equations of the line tangent to the graph of \(\vr\) when \(t=\pi\text{.}\)
  4. Sketch a plot of the curve \(\vr(t)\) and its tangent line near the point where \(t = \pi\text{.}\) In addition, include a sketch of \(\vr'(\pi)\text{.}\) What is the important role of \(\vr'(\pi)\) in this activity?
Solution.
  1. Since
    \begin{equation*} \vr(\pi) = \cos(\pi) \vi - \sin(\pi) \vj + \pi \vk = -\vi + \pi \vk\text{,} \end{equation*}
    the point on the curve traced out by \(\vr(t)\) when \(t = \pi\) is \((-1,0,\pi)\text{.}\)
  2. The derivative
    \begin{equation*} \vr'(\pi) = -\sin(\pi)\vi - \cos(\pi)\vj + \vk = \vj + \vk \end{equation*}
    is a direction vector for the line tangent to the graph of \(\vr\) at the point where \(t=\pi\text{.}\)
  3. Using the point and the direction vector found in (a) and (b), the parametric equations of the line tangent to the graph of \(\vr\) when \(t=\pi\) are
    \begin{equation*} x(t) = -1, \ \ \ y(t) = t, \ \ \ z(t) = \pi+t\text{.} \end{equation*}
