Preview Activity 10.1.1.

In this activity we consider how we might use vectors to define a curve in space.
  1. On a single set of axes in \(\R^2\text{,}\) draw the vectors
    • \(\left\langle \cos(0), \sin(0) \right\rangle\text{,}\)
    • \(\left\langle \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right), \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) \right\rangle\text{,}\)
    • \(\left\langle \cos\left(\pi\right), \sin\left(\pi\right) \right\rangle \text{,}\) and
    • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) \right\rangle\)
    with their initial points at the origin.
  2. On the same set of axes, draw the vectors
    • \(\left\langle \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\text{,}\)
    • \(\left\langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\text{,}\)
    • \(\left\langle \cos\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\text{,}\) and
    • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\)
    with their initial points at the origin.
  3. Based on the pictures from parts (a) and (b), sketch the set of terminal points of all of the vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from 0 to \(2 \pi\text{.}\) What is the resulting figure? Why?
  4. Suppose we sketched the terminal points of all vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from 0 to \(\pi\text{.}\) How does the resulting picture differ from the one in part (c)? What about for \(t\) from 0 to \(4 \pi\text{?}\)
Solution.
  1. The first vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((1, 0)\text{.}\) The second vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((0, 1)\text{.}\) The third vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((-1, 0)\text{.}\) The fourth vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((0, -1)\text{.}\)
  2. The first vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})\text{.}\) The second vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})\text{.}\) The third vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})\text{.}\) The fourth vector has an initial point of \((0, 0)\) and terminal point \((\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})\text{.}\)
  3. The resulting picture is a circle traced counterclockwise with radius \(1\text{,}\) an initial point at \((1, 0)\text{,}\) and terminal point at \((1, 0)\text{.}\)
  4. The resulting picture is a semicircle traced counterclockwise with radius \(1\text{,}\) an initial point at \((1, 0)\text{,}\) and terminal point at \((-1, 0)\text{.}\)
    If the bounds changed to \(t = 0\) to \(t = 4 \pi\text{,}\) the same clockwise circle with radius \(1\) would be traced a second time for \(t = 2 \pi\) to \(t = 4 \pi\text{.}\)
