Preview Activity 10.1.1.

In this activity we consider how can use vectors to define a curve in space.

(a)

On a single set of axes in \(\R^2\text{,}\) draw the vectors below with their initial points at the origin:
  • \(\displaystyle \langle{\cos(0), \sin(0)}\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle{\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}), \sin(\frac{\pi}{2})}\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos(\pi), \sin(\pi) \rangle \)
  • \(\displaystyle \\langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right)\rangle\)

(b)

On the same set of axes, draw the vectors with their initial points at the origin:
  • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \left\langle \cos\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right) \right\rangle\)

(c)

Based on the pictures from parts (a) and (b), sketch the set of terminal points of all of the vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from 0 to \(2 \pi\text{.}\) What is the resulting figure? Why?

(d)

Suppose we sketched the terminal points of all vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from 0 to \(\pi\text{.}\) How does the resulting picture differ from the one in part (c)? What about for \(t\) from 0 to \(4 \pi\text{?}\)
