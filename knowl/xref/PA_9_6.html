Preview Activity 10.1.1.

In this activity, we will consider how to use vectors to define a curve in space.

(a)

Compute each of the following vectors and draw each of these vectors with initial point at the origin on the same set of axes.
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos(0), \sin(0) \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right), \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos(\pi), \sin(\pi) \rangle \)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right)\rangle\)

(b)

Compute the following vectors and plot the results on the same set of axes as part (a) with their initial points at the origin.
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right) \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) \rangle\)
  • \(\displaystyle \langle \cos\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right), \sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{4}\right) \rangle\)

(c)

Based on the plot of results from parts (a) and (b), sketch the set of terminal points of all of the vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from \(0\) to \(2 \pi\text{.}\) You should plot just the terminal points and not the arrows/vectors. Write a couple sentences that state what shape your figure is and why this is what is traced out by the terminal points of \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{.}\)

(d)

How would the plot of the terminal points of all vectors of the form \(\langle \cos(t), \sin(t) \rangle\text{,}\) where \(t\) assumes values from 0 to \(\pi\) be different than your answer to part (c)? How would the figure change if we considered \(t\) from 0 to \(4 \pi\text{?}\)
