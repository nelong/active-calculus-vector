Activity 13.3.4.

Let \(\vF(x,y) = \langle y^2,2xy+3\rangle\text{.}\)
(a)
Let \(C_1\) be the portion of the graph of \(y=2x^3+3x^2-12x-15\) from \((-2,5)\) to \((3,30)\text{.}\) Calculate \(\int_{C_1}\vF\cdot d\vr\text{.}\)
Solution.
If \(\vF=\langle y^2,2xy+3\rangle \) and the curve \(C_1\) is traced out by \(\vr(t) = \langle t, 2t^3+3t^2-12t-15 \rangle \) for \(-2 \leq t\leq 3 \text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_1} \vF\cdot d\vr =\int_{-2}^3 28t^{6} + 72t^{5} - 195t^{4} - 528 t^{3} + 180 t^{2} + 738 t + 189\, dt = 2825 \end{equation*}
(b)
Let \(C_2\) be the line segment from \((-2,5)\) to \((3,30)\text{.}\) Calculate \(\int_{C_2}\vF\cdot d\vr\text{.}\)
Solution.
In this part, the curve \(C_2\) is traced out by \(\vr(t) =\langle -2+5t,5+25t \rangle\) for \(0 \leq t\leq 1\text{.}\) So
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_2} \vF\cdot d\vr =\int_{0}^1 \langle (5+25t)^2,2(-2+5t)(5+25t)+3\rangle \cdot \langle 5,25\rangle \,dt = 2825 \end{equation*}
(c)
Let \(C_3\) be the circle of radius \(3\) centered at the origin, oriented counterclockwise. Calculate \(\oint_{C_3} \vF\cdot d\vr\text{.}\)
Solution.
In this part, the curve \(C_3\) is traced out by \(\vr(t) =\langle 3\cos(t),3\sin(t) \rangle\) as \(0 \leq t\leq 2\pi\text{.}\) So
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_3} \vF\cdot d\vr =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \langle 9(\sin(t))^2,2\cos(t)\sin(t)+3 \rangle \cdot \langle -3\sin(t),3\cos(t) \rangle \, dt = 0 \end{equation*}
(d)
To connect the previous parts of this activity, use a graphing utility to plot the curves \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) on the same axes.
(i)
What type of curve is \(C_1 - C_2\text{?}\)
Solution.
Since the curves have the same endpoints but \(-C_2\) is oriented oppositely, \(C_1-C_2\) forms a closed curve.
(ii)
What is the value of \(\oint_{C_1-C_2}\vF\cdot d\vr\text{?}\)
Solution.
Since \(\int_{C_1}\vF\cdot d\vr = \int_{C_2}\vF\cdot d\vr\text{,}\) we have that
\begin{equation*} \oint_{C_1 - C_2}\vF\cdot d\vr = \int_{C_1}\vF\cdot d\vr - \int_{C_2}\vF\cdot d\vr = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
(iii)
What does your answer to partÂ c allow you to say about the value of the line integral of \(\vF\) along the top half of \(C_3\) compared to the line integral of \(\vF\) from \((3,0)\) to \((-3,0)\) along the bottom half of the circle of radius \(3\) centered at the origin?
Solution.
If we reverse the orientation of the bottom half, these curves combine to form \(C_3\text{.}\) Thus, the sum of the two line integrals is \(0\text{,}\) and so when changing the orientation of the bottom portion of the circle, we get that the line integrals are equal.
