Activity 13.11.4.

Because Stokes’ Theorem requires us to consider a surface (with normal vector) and the boundary of the surface, this activity will give you a chance to practice identifying the boundary of some surfaces in \(\R^3\text{.}\) For each surface below:
  1. Describe the boundary in words.
  2. Find a parametrization for the boundary.
  3. Ensure that a person walking along the boundary in the direction of your parametrization with head pointing in the direction of the surface’s normal vector would hold their left hand over the surface.
(a)
The surface \(S_1\) is the portion of the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=4\) with \(z\geq x\text{.}\) Assume the outward orientation on the sphere.
Solution.
  1. The boundary is the circle on the plane \(z=x\) of radius 2, centered at the origin.
  2. \(\vr(t) = \langle 2 \cos(t),2\sin(t),2\cos(t) \rangle\) with \(t \in [0,2\pi)\)
  3. Using the right hand rule on this orientation will show that the circulation on the boundary will be measured with the positive z-component (corresponding to the outward normal of the sphere)
(b)
The surface \(S_2\) is the portion of the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=4\) with \(z\geq 0\text{.}\) Assume the outward orientation on the sphere.
Solution.
  1. The boundary is the circle on the plane \(z=0\) of radius 2, centered at the origin.
  2. \(\vr(t) = \langle 2 \cos(t),2\sin(t),0 \rangle\) with \(t \in [0,2\pi)\)
  3. Using the right hand rule on this orientation will show that the circulation on the boundary will be measured with the positive z-component (corresponding to the outward normal of the disc in the xy-plane)
(c)
The surface \(S_3\) is the portion of the hyperbolic paraboloid \(z=x^2-y^2\) with \(x^2+y^2\leq 1\text{.}\) Assume the “upward” orientation, e.g., the normal vector at \((0,0,0)\) is \(\vk\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. The boundary is the path that sits above the circle of radius 1 in the xy-plane centered at the origin, but has z-coordinate given by \(\cos(2 \theta)\text{.}\)
  2. \(\vr(t) = \langle \cos(t),\sin(t),\cos(2t) \rangle\) with \(t \in [0,2\pi)\)
  3. Using the right hand rule on this orientation will show that the circulation on the boundary will be measured with the positive z-component (corresponding to the outward normal of the saddle surface)
(d)
The surface \(S_4\) is the portion of the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=4\) for which \(-2\leq z\leq 2\text{,}\) assuming the outward orientation.
Hint.
It is fine for the boundary of a surface to be made up of more than one curve. Think carefully about how each piece is oriented!
Solution.
  1. The boundary is given by the curve \(C\) consisting of (given in order of the orientation) the quarter-circle of radius \(2\) centered at \((2,0,-2)\) in the plane \(z=-2\) from \((2,0,-2)\) to \((0,2,-2)\text{,}\) the line segment from \((0,2,-2)\) to \((0,2,2)\text{,}\) the quarter-circle of radius \(2\) centered at \((0,0,2)\) in the plane \(z=2\) from \((0,2,2)\) to \((2,0,2)\text{,}\) and the line segment from \((2,0,2)\) to \((2,0,-2)\text{.}\)
  2. \begin{align*} \vr(t) \amp = \langle 2\cos(t),2\sin(t),-2 \rangle \\ \vr(t) \amp = \langle 2,0,4-t \rangle \\ \vr(t) \amp = \langle 2\cos(-t),2\sin(-t),2 \rangle \\ \vr(t) \amp = \langle 2,0,-2+t \rangle \end{align*}
    with the first and third having \(t\in[0,\pi/2]\) and the second and fourth having \(t\in[0,4]\text{.}\)
  3. Using the right hand rule on this orientation will show that the circulation on the boundary will be measured in the radially outward direction (away from the z-axis
