Properties of Scalar Line Integrals.

For a constant scalar \(k\text{,}\) scalar valued functions\(f\) and \(g\text{,}\) and oriented curves \(C\text{,}\) \(C_1\text{,}\) and \(C_2\text{,}\) the following properties hold:
  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_C (k f) \, ds = k \int_C f \, ds\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_C (f+g) \, ds = \int_C f \, ds + \int_C g \, ds \)
  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_{-C} f \, ds = \int_C f \, ds\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_{C_1+C_2} f \, ds = \int_{C_1} f \, ds + \int_{C_2} f \, ds\)
